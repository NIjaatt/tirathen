_A world shaped by catastrophe but held together by ambition, faith and stubbornness._

## The World
Tirathen is a living world orbiting a yellow sun, its two moons visible in the night sky. The continent most relevant to you is **Valloryn**. A vast landmass of ancient kingdoms, ruined empires, and uneasy borders.

Tirathen is not a world at peace. It is a world scarred by disasters it has not fully recovered from, and bracing for ones on the horizon. Magic is not native here. It arrived with the Humans, and it nearly killed everything. That wound above all, has not been forgotten.

## A History in Broad Strokes
**The Age of Founding** saw the first civilisations rise. Chief among them was the **Obellian Empire**, a now-fallen civilisation of extraordinary mechanical ingenuity whose ruins still dot the continent and whose legacy walks among the living in the form of reawakening **Automatons**.

**The Age of Calamity** brought the **Dooms of Tirathen**, five titanic monsters of divine origin that rampaged across the lands for nearly a century. They were not all defeated, only driven back. Some are dormant. Heroes who rose to fight them ascended to become gods, forming the **Heroic Pantheon**. Their ascension ushered the world into the **Age of Legend**.

**The Age of Suffering** began when Humans arrived through a portal from another world, bringing with them **magic**, a force Tirathen's peoples were not physiologically able to hold. It spread like a plague and killed roughly eight in ten of the native population. This catastrophe is called the **Spellplague**, and it reshapes politics to the present day. The Humans, unbothered by magic in their own bodies, used it to conquer the Elvish nation they landed on. Forcing the Elves to retreat to their homeland, the Isle of Valeniir.

**The Age of Darkness** followed when an asteroid struck the moon **Belegyr**, sending fragments raining onto the world and blocking out the sun for five hundred years. It destroyed civilisations that had barely started to recover.

**The Age of Adventure** is now. The sun has been back for over a thousand years. Nations have rebuilt, or tried to. And something is stirring again, felt in the air and the shifting currents of the seas, erratic monsters and animals, and winters that do not end.

## The Gods
**The Celestial Pantheon** are the original creator gods, the **Eight Makers** who shaped the world's peoples and the **Four Watchers** who observed. They rarely intervene directly, but their influence shapes every culture.

**The Heroic Pantheon** are ascended mortals, worshipped for their deeds against the Dooms and other great evils. They are more involved, more accessible, and more beloved in most cultures.

**The Dark Pantheon** are gods of corruption and ruin, creators of monsters, demons, and the unnatural. They are not merely evil in the abstract: their servants are active in the world.

**The Old Pantheon** are the four elemental gods: wind, fire, earth, and sea. They are considered the oldest religion on the continent.

Outside all of these is **Nhalgorath**, the Great Destroyer, an imprisoned outer god sealed inside the shattered moon of Belegyr. Every pantheon, dark gods included, agrees it must never be released.

The dominant organised religion is the **Octavian Faith**, a powerful church built around the Eight Makers and headquartered in the Holy Kingdom of Halmyr. Its influence reaches across most of Valloryn in the form of cathedrals, schools, crusaders, and courts.

## The Peoples
Tirathen is home to an enormous variety of peoples. What follows is a broad overview.

**Humans** arrived from another world and are now the most populous ancestry on the continent. Adaptable, ambitious, and mistrusted by many for what their arrival cost the native peoples.

**Elves** are ancient, long-lived, and largely absent. They retreated to their island homeland of **Valeniir** during the Spellplague and have barely left since. A few have recently begun re-entering the world after over a millennia of isolation.

**Dwarves and Gnomes** live primarily underground in the **Dunvaar Commonwealth**, a secretive and extraordinarily advanced technocracy. Their craftsmanship has no equal, and their true capabilities are known only to a few.

**Beastfolk** are a broad category of sentient, bipedal animal peoples: catfolk, hyenalike Kholo, ratfolk, tengu, lizardfolk, and froglike Tripkee. Most live in **Myrathin**, a democratic republic built after a revolution that overthrew a corrupt monarchy. They have faced slavery and persecution across Valloryn, and continue to in some nations.

**Orcs** are a physically powerful people native to the south, shaped by conflict and defined by codes of honesty and strength. Many work as mercenaries after their homeland was colonised by a religious empire.

**Goblins and Hobgoblins** have complex histories. Goblins like Orcs are a people from the southern lands. Hobgoblins are their engineered creations. Both navigate a world that has rarely welcomed them.

**Athamaru** are fish-like humanoids who recently emerged from the sea, displaced from the deep ocean by something unknown. They live on a large island and are only beginning to understand what life on land means.

**Automatons** are ancient mechanical beings from the Obellian Empire, housing souls inside construct bodies. Most have been awake for thousands of years and struggle to maintain their sense of self.

Several other peoples exist: halflings, kobolds, centaurs, and stranger things besides.

## The Nations
**The Korvian Empire** is the largest power on Valloryn. A vast, militaristic Human empire built on conquered Elven lands. Currently in an uneasy peace following a twenty-year war with its neighbour. Beastfolk slavery is practiced here.

**The Kingdom of Valdyr** is a small but fierce absolutist monarchy that has held its own against Korvia for centuries. It was recently split in two when the king's own son defected to form a breakaway state.

**The Elovanite Principality** is that breakaway state, barely three years old, founded on a moral rupture rather than ambition. It is fragile, held together by shared ideology, and surrounded by forces that could end it.

**The Holy Kingdom of Halmyr** is a theocratic kingdom and the seat of the Octavian Faith. It has launched three religious crusades in its history and governs with divine authority. Its influence reaches well beyond its borders.

**The Ardian Protectorate** is Halmyr's colonial vassal, maintained to contain a sealed Lich King beneath the ruins of a conquered Orc nation. The original inhabitants live under occupation.

**The Republic of Myrathin** is a young democratic republic built by Beastfolk after a revolution, still navigating the tensions between its founding ideals and an increasingly fractious political landscape.

**The Dunvaar Commonwealth** is an underground Dwarf and Gnome technocracy sitting on technology centuries ahead of anyone else. Neutral, secretive, and in possession of weapons they refuse to sell.

**The City-State of Karreth** is a floating city above the Shifting Sands, home to the world's foremost school of magic. Politically neutral, indispensable to every nation.

**The Fourfold Dominion** is a pirate thalassocracy ruling the Emerald Sea. Four pirate lords unified and fought a naval war to decide who would lead. One won. The others serve. They tax every ship that sails through their waters, and no nation can stop them.

**The Free Tribes** are a newly unified coalition of northern peoples fleeing a catastrophic freeze, camped at the border of Korvia. Their High Chieftain, an Orc named **Valdris**, forged this coalition in the last few years from peoples who have always been enemies. Whether it holds together is still an open question.

**Valeniir** is the Elven island nation. Ancient, isolated, self-sufficient, and guarded by magical wards that have held for eight hundred years. The Elves are only now, cautiously, beginning to look outward again.

**Nirryth** is a small island territory settled by the Athamaru eight years ago. It is not yet a proper nation. It is a people deciding what it wants to become.

## The Present
It is the year **1345 A.C**. The world is changing in sudden and drastic ways that are easier to feel than to name.

The oceans are behaving strangely. The north is freezing beyond what any living generation has known. Ancient creatures are stirring. The political order is tense but standing. For now.
