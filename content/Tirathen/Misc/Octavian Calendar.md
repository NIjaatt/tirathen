The Octavian Calendar is the most commonly used calendar in [[Valloryn]]. In this calendar, the day of the month is always listed first, followed by the month and the year, generally suffixed by the current Era.

### Origin
The Octavian Calendar takes its origin from the [[the Age of Calamity|Age of Calamity]]. The early founders of the [[Octavian Faith]] drew this calendar from the rhythms of the two moons and the turn of the seasons. They anchored its days and months to the names of the [[Celestial Pantheon]] as an act of devotion and gratitude for their survival. As the Faith spread across Valloryn in the centuries that followed, so too did the calendar, adopted first by the nations that fell under Octavian influence and eventually by almost all peoples of the continent.

### Eras Abbreviated
There are three main abbreviated suffixes that accompany the years on the Octavian Calendar. Firstly there is P.C which stands for Pre-Calamity. This Era represents the time before the [[the Dooms of Tirathen|Dooms of Tirathen]] roamed the lands. Secondly, there is P.D which stands for Post Doom. This Era represents the time after the Dooms of Tirathen were defeated and vanquished. Finally and most recently, there is A.C which stands for After Collapse. This Era represents the time after the [[the Great Collapse|Great Collapse]].

### Days, Weeks, Months, and Seasons
In the Octavian Calendar, each day on [[Tirathen]] has 24 hours, each week has seven days, and each year has 53 weeks. A year is divided into 4 months, one month for every season.

#### Days of the Week
Although most days of the week are assigned for work, a few also have other meanings in most cultures. The Octavian Faith generally holds a religious ceremony every day dedicated to the particular Maker god the day is named for.

- **Asday:** Named after [[Asurion]]. Generally a work day.
- **Arday:** Named after [[Barúm]]. Generally a work day.
- **Lurday:** Named after [[Felurion]]. Generally a work day.
- **Fenday:** Named after [[Fenrith]]. Generally a work day.
- **Lynday:** Named after [[Orilyn]]. Generally a work day.
- **Adiday:** Named after [[Radia]]. Generally a day of rest.
- **Tharday:** Named after [[Sarithar]]. Generally a day of rest.

#### Seasons
The known world of Tirathen has four seasons: Summer, Fall, Winter and Spring.
