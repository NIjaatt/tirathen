_Lizardfolk are scaled humanoids with a history that stretches into the distant past._

Lizardfolk are consummate survivors, heirs to empires considered ancient even by the oldest of ancestries. Lizardfolk move through the societies of other humanoids with the steely reserve of born predators. They have a well-deserved reputation as outstanding rangers and unsentimental fighters.

Though lizardfolk have adapted to many different environments, many of them still prefer to remain near bodies of water, using their ability to hold their breath to their advantage. As a result, they usually prefer equipment that is not easily damaged by moisture, eschewing leather and metal for gear made of stone, ivory, glass, and bone.

### Lizardfolk Typically
- Hold their people's history in high regard and look to the past for solutions to present problems.
- Strive to adapt perfectly to their environment while also keeping their culture and traditions intact.

### Other Ancestries Probably...
- Assume Lizardfolk are beholden to tradition and have ancient knowledge.
- See Lizardfolk as cold-blooded and callous due to their subdued physical reactions.

### Physical Description
Lizardfolk vary depending on their environment, but share toothy snouts and long, powerful tails. Many sport dorsal spikes or flashy neck frills that hint at their clan lineage.

Lizardfolk are primarily swamp-dwellers, and are good swimmers. In the water, they primarily propel themselves with their tails in a manner reminiscent of an alligator.

Lizardfolk are cold-blooded, and cannot regulate their body temperature internally. As such, they are primarily diurnal and become sluggish during the night. They are also intolerant of low ambient temperatures, leading to their preference for warm and temperate environments.

Lizardfolk reach physical adulthood at age 15 and live up to 120 years. The average lizardfolk stands 6 to 7 feet tall, but will continue to grow throughout their lifetime, gaining strength and size with age.

Unlike their [[awakened animal]] cousins, Lizardfolk do not go through the process of awakening. Instead, they have intelligence from birth. A distinction that separates all the six main [[beastfolk]] from awakened animals.

### Beliefs
Most Lizardfolk are unconcerned with heavy questions of morality and focus their efforts on a local level.

Lizardfolk are largely isolationist, and do not leave their territories without a specific purpose in mind. They tend to distrust [[human|humans]] due to their history of conflict over land and [[Elf|elves]] due to an unspecified betrayal spoken of in lizardfolk legend.

### Society
Known among themselves as Iruxi, lizardfolk are raised communally from the moment they break from their shells. Their traditions stretching back thousands of years, brought to life through epic poems, evocative carvings, and ancestral rites performed among fields of fossilized bone. Lizardfolk are passionate astrologers with one eye on the future. If they seem slow to act, it's because their long history has taught them the value of patience.

Lizardfolk run their tribes using a style of kraterocracy, where might makes right. Their tribes are usually ruled by their strongest and most aggressive member, regardless of gender.

True iruxi settlements are often overlooked, as they are partially or mostly submerged in water. Lizardfolk bones often adorn the walls, as many lizardfolk believe these remains can be animated by ancestral spirits when the residents are in danger.

#### Language
Many Lizardfolk speak Iruxi, a language made up of deep guttural groans and growls made possible by their unique vocal cords and thick chests.

#### Religion
Iruxi religion plays a large role in their culture, but in a heavily practical way, blending animism and ancestor worship with druidic rites. Of the gods, only their creator god, [[Orilyn]] is commonly revered.

Religious life is typically led by shamans, who often pierce themselves with animal bones to symbolize their close connection to nature. Although shamans are highly influential figures in lizardfolk societies, they rarely lead tribes in their own right.
