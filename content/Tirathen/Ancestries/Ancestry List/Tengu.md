_Tengus are gregarious and resourceful avian humanoids who collect knowledge and treasures alike._

All Tengu wander [[Tirathen]] in search of a better life, bringing their skill with blade crafting to lands far from their home. In maritime regions, tengu notably work as fishers, blacksmiths, and "jinx eaters"; members of ships' crews who are believed, accurately or otherwise, to absorb misfortune. Having lived in a variety of conditions and locations, tengu tend to be nonjudgmental, though their willingness to associate with lawbreakers has often led some to regard them with suspicion.

### Tengu Typically
- Are gregarious and eager to find a flock of their own.
- Voraciously absorb the practices of those around them, sometimes even forgetting where they came from.

### Other Ancestries Probably...
- Get confused by a Tengus simultaneous respect for and disregard of tradition.
- Have trouble reading a Tengus expressions or regard them with suspicion and superstition.

### Physical Description
Tengu have many avian characteristics. Their faces are tipped with sharp beaks and their scaled forearms and lower legs end in talons. As closed footwear tends to fit poorly unless custom made, many tengu wear open sandals or simply go barefoot. Tengu are rarely more than 5 feet tall, and they are even lighter than their smaller frames would suggest, as they have hollow bones. Tengu have vestigial wings, which are incapable of natural flight, though there are occasionally legends of Tengu achieving natural flight.

Tengu have shorter lifespans than [[human|humans]]. They reach maturity at around 12 years old and can live up to 60, though many rarely reach such an age due to their lifestyles.

Tengu, like birds, reproduce by laying and inseminating eggs. However, apart from their reproduction cycle, beaks, feathers, and taloned feet, they have humanoid traits like fingered hands with opposable thumbs, and plantigrade legs.

Unlike their [[awakened animal]] cousins, Tengu do not go through the process of awakening. Instead, they have intelligence from birth. A distinction that separates all the six main [[beastfolk]] from awakened animals.

### Beliefs
Tengu revere their elders, finding long-lived tengu just as qualified to lead tengu society as highly experienced tengu.

A tengu who leaves their ancestral homeland feels an intense pull toward adventure, to cross vast distances, collect beautiful treasures, and brave the challenges of combat or the rolling sea.

Tengu are survivalists and conversationalists, equally at home living off the wilderness and finding a niche in dense cities. They accumulate knowledge, tools, and companions, adding them to their collection as they travel.

### Society
The greatest divide in tengu society is between tengu remaining in their ancestral home and those who have dispersed across the world. Tengu refer to these two groups as those "in the roost" and those "migrating" respectively.

Roosted tengu tend to be more traditionalist and conservative and are especially concerned with preserving their culture in the face of years of erosion from oppression. Migrating tengu, on the other hand, voraciously absorb the culture of the various nations and settlements that they now call home.

#### Language
Tengu speak the Tengu language which consists of various cawks, cries and stamping of feet.

#### Religion
Tengu often follow the faith of the region in which they were raised, though many worship their creator god, [[Orilyn]].
