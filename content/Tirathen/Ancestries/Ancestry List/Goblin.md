_Goblins are a short, scrappy, energetic people who have spent millennia maligned and feared._

The convoluted histories other people cling to don't interest goblins. These small folk live in the moment, and they prefer tall tales over factual records. Goblin virtues are about being present, creative, and honest. They strive to lead fulfilled lives, rather than worrying about how their journeys will end. To tell stories, not nitpick the facts. To be small, but dream big. 

Many goblins enjoy simpler delights like songs, fire, and eating, and hate reading, dogs, and horses. Other goblins might have more complex pursuits, though, such as tinkering with scraps or concocting snacks and explosives from most anything.

### Goblins Typically
- Strive to prove to other's and themselves that they have a place among other civilized peoples.
- Lighten the heavy emotional burdens others carry (and amuse themselves) with antics and pranks.

### Other Ancestries Probably...
- Work to ensure Goblins don't accidentally (or intentionally) set too many things on fire.
- Wonder how they've survived given their typical gastronomic choices, reckless behaviour, and love of fire.

### Physical Description
Goblins are stumpy humanoids with large bodies, scrawny limbs, and massively oversized heads with large ears and beady red eyes. Their skin ranges from green to gray to blue, and they often bear scars, boils, and rashes. Goblins average 3 feet tall. Most are bald, with little or no body hair.

Goblins' extremely high level of energy comes from both their accelerated metabolism. Which makes goblins eating machines stricken with almost constant hunger, fortunately, goblins can subsist on almost anything organic. They love salty foods, especially pickled food of all types, but detest fresh vegetables. Their jagged teeth fall out and regrow constantly to avoid decay from their constant eating.

It's quite hard to distinguish goblin genders, though they do possess reproductive organs. Excessive weight is considered a sign of beauty among feminine goblins.

Goblins mature rapidly, reaching adulthood in seven to eight years. Living up to 50 years or more, but due to their reckless and dangerous lifestyles, few live past 20 years of age.

### Beliefs
Even the most well-intentioned goblins have trouble following the rules, meaning goblin adventurers are often unsure whether they're on the right side of the law.

Many are also awed by magic and magic users, and greatly respect goblins who can harness magic. They are also generally superstitious in nature, and many exhibit a particular fascination with fire.

Goblins have a near-pathological fear and hatred of horses and dogs, most likely due to the mutual hatred displayed by these animals. Goblins are, in many ways, defined by their fears, and not just of dogs and horses but also of humans, for even their religion is based on fear of their deities.

Goblins love to collect treasure, but value appearance over utility. Thus, a shiny but worthless trinket is valued over a powerful but ugly magic item. Their love of shiny treasure means that goblins are also a common sight in the refuse pits and junkyards of non-goblin settlements throughout the lands, where they scavenge for items they can craft into weapons and other interesting tools, a task they are unusually proficient in.

### Society
Goblins tend to flock to strong leaders, forming small tribes. These tribes rarely number more than a hundred, though the larger a tribe is, the more diligent the leader must be to keep order—a notoriously difficult task. Play and creativity matter more to goblins than productivity or study, and their encampments erupt with songs and laughter. 

Goblins rarely build their own structures; they prefer to use terrain and natural defences to their advantage, often settling in areas of heavy undergrowth, easily defensible caves, and ruins. They rely on scavenging for most of their sustenance and are gifted at surviving in even the most hostile climes.
  
Goblins bond closely with their allies, fiercely protecting those companions who have protected them or offered a sympathetic ear. Goblins tend to assume for their own protection that members of taller ancestries, which goblins often refer to colloquially as “longshanks,” won't treat them kindly. Learning to trust longshanks is difficult for a goblin, and it's been only in recent years that such a partnership has even been an option.

Goblins keep their names simple. A good name should be easy to pronounce, short enough to shout without getting winded, and taste good to say. The namer often picks a word that rhymes with something they like so that writing songs is easier.

#### Language
Goblins speak Goblin, the language not possessing a written script. Typically a Goblin tribe will have one speaker of Trade Common.

#### Religion
Most Goblins are aware of their creator god, [[Seralyn]] and their relationship, but feel no personal attachment or need to worship her. Most Goblins would rather pick their own deities, choosing powerful monsters, natural wonders, or anything else they find fascinating. Sometimes even attributing deific status to fellow goblins, though they were likely duped into believing so.

The most widely worshipped god by Goblins is [[Vrakul]], a Goblin that ascended to divinity and a member of the [[Heroic Pantheon]]. He is known and worshipped as the strongest and coolest Goblin to ever exist. Many Goblins follow his creed and rulership by creating allegiances within the different tribes and paying as much respect as possible to the chain of command.
