_Ratfolk are small, clever, and adaptable humanoids with ratlike features and a love of community._

Ysoki are beings that most surface-dwelling humanoids refer to as "ratfolk." They are a communal people who prefer cramped conditions, with up to 100 individuals living in a given home. If they can't find homes in town, ratfolk may instead live in caves and cavern complexes, as these provide great storage for the many and varied goods they bring back from trading expeditions.

Ratfolk love to travel, and they can often be found on the road in merchant caravans. They're good with their hands and have a keen eye for spotting anything out of the ordinary, from threats to treasures. They're also inveterate hoarders; ysoki warrens are crammed full of unusual odds and ends gathered by previous generations and stowed away in preparation for some future emergency.

### Ratfolk Typically
- Have pride in their large extended family, and try to stay in contact with everyone in it.
- Like to travel near and far in search of new experiences, likely collecting trinkets along the way.

### Other Ancestries Probably...
- Are surprised by a Ratfolk's careful grooming and other fastidious habits.
- Think Ratfolk are crowding them and invading their personal space.

### Physical Description
Ratfolk have whiskered snouts, large ears, and hairless tails, and typically have red eyes and short brown or black fur. A common ratfolk is 4 feet tall and weighs around 80 pounds. Ratfolk vary considerably, however; even a single family group can exhibit substantial variation in hair colour, eye colour, or size.

Contrary to a common stereotype of being dirty or diseased, ysoki are instinctively clean. This cleanliness is reinforced through their strong social structures. Because their appearance is often considered disturbing by other humanoids, and people may even mistake them for wererats, ratfolk often conceal their physical features with layers of clothing when moving through spaces dominated by other ancestries.

Unlike their [[awakened animal]] cousins, Ratfolk do not go through the process of awakening. Instead, they have intelligence from birth. A distinction that separates all the six main [[beastfolk]] from awakened animals.

### Beliefs
Ratfolk care more about their family than abstract concepts of good and evil. Their warrens might be chaotic dens filled with hoarded knickknacks, but they also obey strict social codes.

### Society
Ysoki culture values cooperation and community. Every ratfolk learns—through communal games, social occasions, and sports; to forge fast friendships and connections with ratfolk outside their family. While they refer to their homes as "warrens", the term is synonymous among them with "home" and can represent anything from a literal cavern to a city tenement, ship, martial arts studio, dungeon, or igloo; any structure that is bustling, dark, and defensible can be a warren.

Ysoki society is also democratic by nature, and while they have a social hierarchy, all voices are heard when making major decisions. Colonies and families within larger warrens often designate a single member as the _swam voice_, who has the authority to govern the warren and resolve disputes, as well as being the final say to major decisions after hearing everyone's voices.

A warren's bone mother serves as its oracle, and when a warren member dies, a bone from their remains is gifted to the bone mother to become part of its physical history.

With a long tradition of working as traders and tinkers, ratfolk travel frequently from one town to another. Their wagons are usually grouped into caravans of up to half a dozen vehicles. They travel widely for at least a year before returning to their warren with spoils aplenty.

Ysoki are extremely loyal to their own kin and are notorious for avenging fallen family members. Threatening one individual is a surefire way to rally the whole community. 

Although not known for their trickery, as they take great pride in their professionalism when dealing with other ancestries, ysoki are not above using their size and numbers to outwit or outmanoeuvre an enemy when threatened.

#### Language
Ratfolk speak the Ysoki language, which is comprised of various clicks, chitters and swishes of their tails.

#### Religion
Though ratfolk revere their ancestors and tend to acknowledge whatever deity is prevalent in their local area. However, many tend to gravitate to their creator god, [[Orilyn]].
