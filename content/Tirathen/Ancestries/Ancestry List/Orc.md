_Orcs are proud, strong people with hardened physiques who value physical might and glory in combat._

Orcs are forged in the fires of violence and conflict, often from the moment they are born. As they live lives that are frequently cut brutally short, orcs revel in testing their strength against worthy foes, often by challenging a higher-ranking member of their community for dominance.

Orcs often struggle to gain acceptance among other communities, who frequently see them as brutes. Those who earn the loyalty of an orc friend, however, soon learn that an orc's fidelity and honesty are unparalleled. Orc culture teaches that they are shaped by the challenges they survive, and the most worthy survive the most hardships. Orcs who attain both a long life and great triumphs command immense respect.

### Orcs Typically
- Eagerly meet any chance to prove their strength in a physical contest.
- View dying in glorious combat as preferable to a mundane death from old age or illness.

### Physical Description
Orcs are tall and powerfully built, with long arms and stocky legs. Many orcs top 7 feet in height, though they tend to adopt broad, almost bow-legged stances and slouch forward at the shoulders. They posses sharp, boar-like canines that jut out from their mouth, which allows for their unique language to be spoken.

Orcs have rough skin, thick bones, and rock-hard muscles, making them suited to war and other physically demanding tasks. Orc skin tones can vary between a light gray through various shades of green as an adaptation to their environment, though most are some shade of green. Their rough skin scars easily, and even the youngest orc warriors bear an array of scars that they wear with pride.

Masculine and feminine orcs are visibly and structurally alike apart from their reproductive organs. What easily separates the two is that masculine orcs typically are bald and incapable of growing hair on their heads while feminine orcs can.

Orcs consider powerful builds, heavily scarred skin, large tusks, and tattoos attractive, regardless of gender. Orcs reach physical adulthood around the age of 17, with many orcs living to be up to 60 years old.

### Beliefs
A common orc saying is "you are the scars that shape you." Violent, chaotic lives in violent, chaotic lands mean that most orcs tend to expect and accept violence as a part of their everyday lives.

Honesty is a key virtue across many orc cultures, and deception is considered despicable cowardice. Minor lies, half-truths, and omissions—even in service of diplomacy—are particularly despised. With acute knowledge of how short their lives can be, most orcs also value efficient decisiveness.

Orcs have an incredibly high pain threshold. They experience pain as vividly as any other sentient race but possess a near unparalleled stoicism. Orcs embrace pain in ways that allow them to survive agonies that would break or kill other creatures, and many both enhance and demonstrate this through intricate tattoos and scarification.

#### Kromshatar
Orcs believe that if a creature has a face and a name, it can be killed. Some orc holds teach that the greatest members of the hold can earn a chance to challenge the gods for a place amid their pantheons. This challenge is called Kromshatar. This is indicated by the appearance of a Vow Mark illuminated on the corpse of a deceased orc, which indicates that the orc's soul had entered the mythical battle that is Kromshatar to challenge a deity in single combat to the death. A mortal winner ascends to divinity; defeat completely annihilates their soul. Though no orc in recorded history has ever won Kromshatar against a deity.

### Society
Most orc communities, known as holds, define themselves through two things: pain and glory. Each earns respect in near equal measure, so long as the pain is borne with stoicism. 

An orc with many scars who walks uncomplaining with a broken leg draws as much admiration as one who wins a great victory on the battlefield. Additionally, power defines the dynamics among families and holds. Weaker orcs work at the behest of the strong, with power constantly shifting between orcs that prove their might.

Orcs tend to share in familial duties, raising children as a community and sharing responsibilities among the entire hold.

#### Language
All Orcs speak a the unique language of Orcish. Their tusks that jut out from their mouths allow for unique sounds typically unproducible by normal mouths. However, a well studied individual can replicate the sounds and learn Orcish with great practice, often drawing surprise and praise from most Orcs.

#### Religion
Many orc communities view religion as simply an exchange of power with whichever deity will help them most in battle. In violent holds and lands, orcs have no interest in any peaceful deities.

Most orcs worship their own ascended god, [[Ulgorak]] the Great Uniter and Chief of Chiefs. Some believe Ulgorak is the paragon of the orc race, raised to divinity by virtue of their sheer orcness, while others believe he was always divine.
