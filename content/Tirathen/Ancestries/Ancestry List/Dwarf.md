_Dwarves are a short, stocky people who are often stubborn, fierce, and devoted._

Dwarves have a well-earned reputation as a stoic and stern people, but they also have an unbridled zeal and deeply value artisanship. They excel at jobs other ancestries find tedious, but also have a great love of exploration and discovery. 

To a stranger, they can seem untrusting and clannish, but to their friends and family, they are warm and caring. While trust from a dwarf is hard-won, once gained it is as strong as iron.

### Dwarves Typically
- Strive to uphold their personal honour and refuse to back down.
- Appreciate quality craftsmanship in all forms and insist upon it for all their gear whether they're an adventurer or not.

### Other Ancestries Probably...
- See Dwarves as stubborn, though whether this is an asset or a detriment changes from moment to moment.
- Recognize the deep connection Dwarves have with their family, heritage, and friends.

### Physical Description
Dwarves are short and stocky, standing about a foot shorter than most humans, but often weighing more. This is due to their wide, compact bodies and burly frames that contain thick, heavy bones, tightly packed muscular sinew.
  
Masculine dwarves tend to be noticeably broader, though not as much taller comparatively, than Feminine dwarves, and have slab-like torsos and heavy eyebrows. Dwarves of all genders can grow facial hair, and do so with pride as having a long beard is viewed as a symbol of honour, strength.

Dwarves typically reach physical adulthood around the age of 25, though their traditionalist culture places more value on completing coming-of-age ceremonies unique to each clan than reaching a certain age. A typical dwarf can live to around 350 years old.

### Beliefs
Dwarves tend to value honour and closely follow the traditions of their clans and kingdoms. They have a strong sense of friendship and justice, though they are often very particular about who they consider a friend. They work hard and play harder—especially when strong ale is involved.

### Society
Dwarves today hold many qualities that propel them to greatness: fierceness, gumption, and stubbornness in their endeavours. Nearly all Dwarves live in the [[Dunvaar|Dunvaar Commonwealth]], where their cities dot the vast mountain ranges both on the surface and deep underground. This creates vast cultural divides between dwarf clans. Yet nearly all dwarven peoples share a passion for stonework, metalwork, and family.

Dwarven cultures place significant importance on honouring their ancestors, including founding figures of their clans and builders of their grand cities, as well as esteemed heroes and skilled artisans. Core virtues such as craftsmanship, mining, smithing, determination, honour, friendship, exploration, and the preservation of family traditions are paramount to dwarves, who seek to prove themselves worthy of their ancestors' esteemed legacy and the divine protection bestowed by their gods.
  
Few dwarves are seen without their clan dagger strapped to their belt. This dagger is forged just before a dwarf's birth and is distinctive to their clan.

#### Language
Most dwarves primarily speak the Dwarven language, although many are also fluent in Trade Common. Dwarven has a runic alphabet that takes it's roots from the Primordial language.

Dwarven names are full of harsh-sounding consonants, and rarely include soft or sibilant sounds. Honorifics are common in given names, such as "-sun" ("-son"), "-dam" ("-daughter"), and "-hild" ("-wife"). Family names sometimes contain words in Common, such as "gold" or "hammer". Also, the letters Q and X do not appear in Dwarven.

#### Religion
Many dwarven cultures are renowned for their deep spirituality and their perspectives on faith that intertwine personal beliefs with ancestral and clan traditions.

Dwarven religious practices are characterized by many ceremonial rites that can often go beyond the edicts or requirements of the deities they worship. These rituals are often group activities, particularly during significant events that commemorate major achievements or the passing of revered heroes, and feature the prominent use of relics expertly crafted from precious metals and lavishly adorned with gemstones. Smaller communities and traveling dwarves more commonly observe condensed and pragmatic rituals, attesting to the adaptability of dwarven faith.

Most dwarves venerate [[Barúm]], the Father of Creation, above all others, although among the dwarves, [[Garethor]], [[Branthor]], and [[Oranthys]] are also given great respect. [[Barúm]]'s teachings still guide most of dwarven culture and thought, as many believe he will abandon them should they ever slack in their duties.

### Attitudes towards other Ancestries
Dwarves are hard-working, dedicated, and tough, and view other races who do not share these traits as being frivolous at best, and weak and degenerate at worst.

Some dwarves consider [[Elf|Elves]] to be the ultimate examples of this, in addition to labelling them as cowards and weaklings for abandoning [[Tirathen]] following [[the Spellplague]] and [[the Great Collapse]] and hiding away on [[Lands of Valeniir|Valeniir]].
