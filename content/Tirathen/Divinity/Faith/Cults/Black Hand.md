The Black Hand is a cult formed around the veneration of Demons.

### Basic Info
**Symbol:** A broken crown half-submerged beneath a flame.
**Key Figures:** First Mouth
**Founded in:** Unknown.

### Background
To the Octavian Faith, to city guards, to the scholars who write about them in careful, frightened prose, they are the Black Hand. The name was coined by the Ministry of Prosecution sometime in the centuries following the First Crusade, derived from the dark rituals that investigators documented at the sites of their gatherings. It is a name meant to diminish and reduce a theological movement to its most grotesque surface details.

Among themselves, they are the False Children. The name carries their entire theology, as they believe that mortals inherited a world that is not theirs. That it belongs to demons, who roamed this world before the Gods stole it from them. One day, with enough faithful hands working in the right places, they will be returned to it.

### History
It's unknown, even by the Black Hand's own followers when the cult was founded. Their relevance only begins in 937 P.D, when Halmyr launched a campaign to purge a cult dedicated to a demon lord operating within the Holy Kingdom's own borders. The purge succeeded, at least in the sense that the cult was driven out, for they were not destroyed. The surviving members relocated north into the Kingdom of Taldar, where Halmyr's reach was weaker and the population more diverse and less scrutinised.

What followed is known history. By 945 P.D, a witch operating within the cult's inner circle, in concert with her demonic patron, tore open the Darkrift. Taldar fell, and the First Crusade was named in 961 P.D, and by 968 P.D the Gates of Ikanos had sealed the corrupted lands away from the rest of Valloryn.

What is less known, is that the cult was not sealed inside Taldar when the Gates went up. Some of its members were already beyond its borders when the wardstones were placed. They survived, and scattered.

#### Survival & Fragmentation
The centuries following the First Crusade were the cult's leanest. Without a territorial base, the Black Hand fragmented into isolated cells operating independently across Valloryn. Contact between cells was infrequent and dangerous. The [[Octavian Faith|Octavian Faith's]] Ministry of Prosecution was and continues to be considerably devoted to hunting them down. They achieved genuine success in some periods, dismantling networks and executing members whose identities had been uncovered.

But the cult's decentralised structure, born of necessity, proved difficult to eradicate entirely. A cell destroyed in one city did not compromise a cell operating in another. Knowledge was passed orally between trusted members, leaving no texts for investigators to seize. They required only believers to survive, and believers, the cult had learned, could be found almost anywhere.

#### Present Day
In 1345 A.C, the Black Hand are small by most measures, but they are no longer merely surviving. Something has changed in the last decade, a renewed sense of purpose moving through the scattered cells, a whisper of coordination that has not been present for generations. New members are being recruited with unusual deliberateness. Old cells that had gone dormant are stirring. The particular attention being paid to the Gates of Ikanos, to those who guard them and to the scholars who study them, has not gone entirely unnoticed by the more attentive agents of the Ministry of Prosecution, though nothing conclusive has yet been assembled.

The Black Hand has recently established something it has not possessed since before the First Crusade: a centre. Not a physical headquarters, or a place that could be found on a map and raided, but a person. A figure known within the cult as the First Mouth, whose identity is known only to the innermost circle of the Black Hand. Under their leadership, the cult has unified their direction for the first time in centuries.

### Core Beliefs
#### The First Claim
The Black Hand believes that Tirathen was not created for its mortal inhabitants. It was created from the demonic, shaped from the same primordial chaos that gave rise to the Abyss, and the beings that emerged from that chaos, demons, were its first and rightful inhabitants. The gods of all pantheons did not build a world from nothing. They stole it, and reshaped Tirathen to suit their own vanity. Casting out its original inhabitants to a plane of imprisonment they call the Abyss. The mortal races placed upon Tirathen afterward are not the inheritors of something sacred. They are the tenants of something stolen.

#### Usurper Gods
The Black Hand does not merely disbelieve in the gods of all faiths. They view them as active wrongdoers, particularly the Eight Makers, who rebuilt the stolen world in their own image and filled it with creatures designed to worship them. The Dark Pantheon is regarded with more complexity. [[Dravathis]], the Demon Queen, whose domain touches the demonic. She receives the cult's particular veneration not as their creator or patron but as the highest living symbol of demon kind.

#### The Rift is Sacred
The opening of the Darkrift is treated as an act of restoration, the first crack in the wall that the gods built to keep the rightful inhabitants of Tirathen locked away. The witch whose name is forgotten to time, is remembered within the cult as a martyr and a saint. The Gates of Ikanos, which sealed the Darkrift's expansion and contained the demonic presence within Taldar, are understood as the greatest current blasphemy in the world, a lock placed by the Octavian Faith on a door that should have been thrown open.

#### The Return
The cult's eschatology is built around a single conviction: that the Gates will fall, the Darkrift will open fully, and the demons will reclaim what was taken from them. The Black Hand do not believe they will be destroyed when this happens. They believe they will be rewarded for their faithfulness, elevated above the mortal population that served the usurper gods, and given a place in the world that follows.

### Organisation
The Black Hand operates in cells, small groups of between five and fifteen members who know each other personally and know almost nothing about the broader organisation. A member arrested and questioned can identify their own cell and nothing further. This structure was adopted out of survival necessity following the First Crusade's aftermath and has never been abandoned.

Each cell is led by a Mouth, a member responsible for theological instruction, ritual leadership, and communication with the cell above them in the hierarchy. Mouths do not know the identities of other Mouths. They communicate upward through a single designated intermediary, known within the cult as a Carrier, whose identity is known to both the cell below and the structure above but who holds no theological authority of their own.

Above the cells sits a small inner council known as the Circle of Eyes, whose members hold a full picture of the cult's present reach and direction. The Circle answers to the First Mouth, whose identity they protect as their most closely held secret.

#### Recruitment
The Black hand does not seek the devout, but rather the useful. People with grievances, with ambitions, with access to things the cult needs. A disaffected soldier stationed near the Gates of Ikanos. A scholar whose career was destroyed by the Ministry of Prosecution on thin evidence. A merchant whose business depends on routes the Octavian Faith has made politically difficult. The theology comes later, introduced gradually, framed not as an invitation to evil but as a revelation of injustice. By the time a recruit understands what they have joined, they are already implicated in it.

#### Practices
Rituals within the Black Hand involve blood sacrifice, demonic summoning, and the ash-markings on their bodies. Ash burned Octavian texts and marked on the skin of participants during rites. These practices are kept entirely hidden from new recruits until trust has been established.
