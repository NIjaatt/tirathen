The Shattered Dawn is a cult formed around the worship of [[Nhalgorath]], the Enemy of All.

### Basic Info
**Symbol:** A shattered circle, its pieces drifting apart.
**Key Figures:** The Herald
**Founded in:** Approximately 1510 P.D, in the immediate aftermath of [[the Great Collapse]]

### Background
The Shattered Dawn are not a religion or even a cult in any conventional sense. They have no theology of salvation, no promised reward, no vision of a world made better by their god's ascendance. What they have is a wound; a fracture in the minds of those who were exposed to the surge of Nhalgorath's influence that leaked from the shattered moon of Belegyr, and a belief system that grew around that wound like scar tissue over the centuries that followed.

To outsiders, the Shattered Dawn represent a categorically different kind of threat compared to the other dangerous cults of the world. Madness, and insanity dressed in the language of revelation, and a plan to end the world of [[Tirathen]].

### History
When the asteroid struck [[Belegyr]] in 1508 P.D and shattered the moon that had served as Nhalgorath's prison for millennia, the divine wards containing the Destroyer did not break entirely. But they cracked. For a period, something leaked through those cracks into the world below. A whisper at the edge of perception that most people felt only as a vague and sourceless dread in those already catastrophic months and forgot about amid everything else the Great Collapse demanded of them.

Some people did not forget, disallowed that luxury. Those most severely affected were typically individuals already psychologically damaged by the collapse itself, people who had lost everything. Exposed to the worst of the impacts and the chaos that followed. In these people, Nhalgorath's leaking influence did not register as dread, but as clarity. As the first thing that had made sense since the sky rained down upon them.

The first communities of what would become the Shattered Dawn formed in the years immediately following [[the Great Collapse]], scattered across [[Valloryn]] in the settlements of survivors. They were not organised, and didn't even know about each other. What they shared was a set of experiences so similar that when they eventually found one another, recognition was immediate: the visions, the compulsions, the sense of being called toward something vast, patient and utterly indifferent to their individual survival, and the strange peace that came it.

#### Formation
The Shattered Dawn's transition from scattered individuals sharing a madness to a unified cult with a functioning hierarchy took almost a century and was largely the work of a single figure whose name has been preserved within the cult as the First Herald. This person was the first member to receive visions from Nhalgorath that were coherent enough to be acted upon rather than merely endured, and that those visions contained the outline of the plan the cult pursues to the present day.

Whether the First Herald was genuinely receiving direction from Nhalgorath or was simply a person whose particular form of madness expressed itself in organisational genius is a question that remains unanswered, and the Shattered Dawn does not ask it. The plan was given, and it will be followed. That it came wrapped in suffering does not make it less true.

#### Present Day
In 1345 A.C, the Shattered Dawn are more organised and more purposeful than at any point in their history. The cult's hierarchy is intact, its leadership is in active communication with what they describe as Nhalgorath's will, and the work of locating and studying [[the Dooms of Tirathen]] has been ongoing for centuries. Progress has been made. The cult knows more about the current locations and states of the surviving Dooms than any other organisation on Tirathen, including the [[Octavian Faith]].

The Shattered Dawn are also growing, not rapidly, but steadily. Every major catastrophe, every war, every famine, every event that breaks people past their capacity to hold themselves together, produces new candidates for the particular fracture that makes a person susceptible to Nhalgorath's pull. [[The Age of Adventure]], for all its name suggests otherwise, has not been short of catastrophes.

### Core Beliefs
#### The World is a Mistake
The Shattered Dawn believe Tirathen should not exist. Nhalgorath existed before creation, and creation was an imposition upon a void that had its own integrity. The gods built something that was never asked for, filled it with suffering, and called it good. Their followers have all experienced enough suffering to find this argument persuasive in a way that is difficult to argue with from the outside.

#### Destruction is Bliss
The ending of the world is not a tragedy, but the resolution of an error. The mortals who die when Nhalgorath is freed are not victims. They are released from a existence that was imposed on them without consent. Death and destruction are to be celebrated and enjoyed. What greater joy can there be but the bliss that follows in the wake of death and destruction?

#### The Sacred Visions
The madness that Nhalgorath's influence produces in its most devoted followers is not treated as an affliction within the cult. It is sacrament, evidence of direct contact with the only true god. A being so vast and so alien that the human mind cannot hold its communication without breaking. Members who have not yet experienced visions are considered uninitiated. Those who receive visions regularly and remain functional enough to act on them are the cult's most revered figures. That the visions accelerate deterioration is understood and accepted. The Shattered Dawn do not expect to survive what they are working toward, and are eager to embrace the sweet release that awaits them.

#### The Will of Doom
The Shattered Dawn understand that the Dooms of Tirathen are not merely Nhalgorath's creations but extensions of its will. Fragments of its consciousness expressed in physical form. To awaken the Dooms is therefore not simply to unleash monsters upon the world. It is to begin the process of reaching Nhalgorath across the barrier of its prison, using its own distributed presence in the world as a bridge. The cult believes that if enough of the Dooms can be awakened simultaneously and directed in a coordinated ritual, the resulting resonance will reach Nhalgorath fully, rousing it from its torpor. Once fully awake, they believe no prison built by gods will hold it.

### Organisation
#### The Herald
The Shattered Dawn are organised around a single a figure known as the Herald, the cult's supreme leader and the individual who currently receives the clearest visions from Nhalgorath. The position is claimed by whoever among the cult demonstrates the most direct and coherent contact with Nhalgorath's will, as evidenced by the specificity and accuracy of their visions. In practice this means the Herald is always someone in an advanced state of psychological deterioration, lucid enough to lead but visibly and unmistakably marked by what they carry.

#### The Shepherds
Beneath the Herald sit the Shepherds, a small council of senior members who have each received visions and retained enough functionality to serve administrative roles. The Shepherd's manage the cult's cells, coordinate the ongoing research into the Dooms, and handle recruitment. They also serve as interpreters of the Herald's visions when those visions become too fragmented to be directly communicated.

#### The Flocks
Ordinary members are organised into cells called a Flock, each led by a Shepherd or a trusted senior member. A member of one Flock knows roughly what other Flocks are working toward, because the cult's doesn't care about any risk of exposure. They believe the benefits of coordination outweighs the risks.

#### Recruitment
Recruitment happens at the edges of catastrophe. The Shattered Dawn maintain a quiet presence near the aftermath of disasters, wars, and famines. As patient observers looking for individuals who are becoming mentally weak in the particular way that suggests susceptibility to Nhalgorath's pull. Initial contact is gentle, almost kind. The Shattered Dawn are genuinely good at recognising suffering, having all experienced it themselves, and the early stages of recruitment feel to the target less like indoctrination and more like being understood for the first time. The real indoctrination comes later, introduced gradually to a mind already softened by grief and the strange comfort of the visions that have, by that point, usually already begun.
