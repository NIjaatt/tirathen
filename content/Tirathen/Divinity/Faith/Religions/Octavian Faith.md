The Octavian Faith is a polytheistic religion formed around the Eight Makers of the [[Celestial Pantheon]]. The High Seat of the Octavian Faith is established in the [[Halmyr|Holy Kingdom of Halmyr]].

### Basic Info
**Symbol:** A Grail within an Eight Pointed Star
**Key People:** The Pope
**Founded in:** 0 P.D
**Current Pope:** [[Asenia Fasro]]

### Background
The Octavian Faith is known to operate across [[Tirathen]], particularly on the continent of [[Valloryn]]. Churches and chapels are present in almost all major settlements, towns and villages in several countries with cathedrals present in most major cities. The church also oversees the vast majority of children's education through the provision of Sunday Schools and classes delivered in rooms attached to the various chapels and churches. It is also common to see musical performances held in church venues.

The Church has a reduced presence on of the continent of [[Telmaria]], relying on other groups to help them complete their duties.

#### Iconography
The main symbol of the church is a golden grail prominently in the centre. Behind the grail is an eight pointed star with two rings circling the grail and the star. It is not uncommon to see this icon in various places within a church, or to see it embellished on the medallions carried by members. Some groups and factions with the church have their own additional iconography. 

#### History
There is little information about the formation of the Octavian Faith, however its first appearance is said to be in the during [[The Age of Calamity]]. In this tumultuous time, they provided the populace, who feared for their lives, a path to peace and comfort. Via faith. One of their notable actions during this time period was dispatching members all across the continent, to endorse various future rulers. Allowing them to later build their cathedrals and convert nations to their religion.

In the centuries after, its influence continued to spread, resulting in it becoming the dominant religion in Tirathen. Eventually, not even nobles and knights could ignore its power anymore.

Despite their intense political power over other countries, it continued to spread influence via providing humanitarian aid and assisting with keeping peace wherever it could. Two notable examples of such was providing aid to and safe passage to the citizens of [[Marqir]] after the sands were corrupted by magic, and the mediation of peace to end [[the War of the Lions]].

### Core Beliefs
#### All Gods are Divine
"In the beginning, the world was formless, shapeless void. There was only darkness and bitter wind. And unto this void, the Great Divine brought light and form. Much there was to do, so the Great Divine divided her soul twenty ways. Forming the first Gods and Goddesses, who worked to give the world shape. When they were done, all were content but eight who saw potential for more. These eight breathed life into the world, and shaped us, their children."

#### The Eight Makers
The central tenet of the Octavian Faith is the belief in the Eight Makers. While the Eight Makers are often associated with protection and blessings, the Faith has also named their Gods as a possible cause of many terrible calamities and catastrophes as a trial to alter mortal kind's perspective on the world.

Whereas the Eight Makers are the central object of worship in the Octavian Faith, there is no doctrine that believers are limited to believing in the Eight Makers. In various places, the Octavian Faith exists alongside indigenous belief systems or has incorporated the beliefs into its social teaching. The Octavian Faith does not forbid the belief in deities other than the Eight Makers.

The Faith has long been a source of art, and this has been true since the dawn of the Octavian Faith. With various depictions of the likeness of the Eight Makers have created over the thousands of years.

The church is known for not contradicting, and in fact incorporating traditional beliefs of the [[Old Faith]], such as the Summer and Winter Solstices, and various minor traditions.

#### Testaments
Most knowledge about the Eight Makers comes from the doctrinal statements recorded in writings known as Testaments. Writing not officially acknowledged by the Faith are referred to as Apocrypha. The known books include: Genesis, Einz, Shala and Redal.

### Organisation
The Octavian Faith is led by the Pope. Under their reign, the Faith is divided into three groups.

#### Ministry of Gospel
The Ministry of Gospel supervises all rituals of the Faith, including the church and Sunday schools. The ministry is overseen by a cardinal.

The Ministry of Gospel constantly works to expand their faith and influence for practical, and secular reasons.

#### The Iscariot Guard
The Iscariot Guard is charged with the defence of the Octavian Faith, and works directly with the Pope. The guards are equipped with holy artifacts and magical weapons and armour, and number around 30,000. The Iscariot Guard are considered amongst one of the deadliest, and most powerful army on Tirathen.

#### Ministry of Prosecution
The Ministry of Prosecution controls the courts of various countries, and oversees investigations into corruption, and crimes committed by members of the church.

The Ministry of Prosecution strongly believes in the strict implementation of doctrines, the rejection of non-believers, and the persecution of heretics.

### Saints
Over the course of its existence, the Octavian Faith has sainted a number of people whose acts where considered woven or directly operated by the hands of the Eight Makers. Below is a list of the most well known saints of the fai

#### Saint Mora
**Ancestry:** Elf
**Time Period:** ~50 P.D

One of the original missionaries dispatched by the Faith during the [[the Age of Calamity|Age of Calamity]], Morra is credited with converting three neighbouring kingdoms to the Octavian Faith before the first cathedral of Halmyr was built. She died on the road, as she had lived, and was sainted for her role in the Faith's early expansion.

#### Saint Torlmond
**Ancestry:** Dwarf
**Time Period:** ~962 P.D

A Dunvaaran master-smith who personally oversaw the forging of the first of the [[Gates of Ikanos]] wardstones during the [[The Holy Crusades|First Crusade]]. It is said he refused to stop working even as demonic forces breached the stronghold where the wardstone was being assembled. With his dying breath, he completed the wardstone. Sainted for sacrificial service in the sealing of the [[the Darkrift|Darkrift]].

#### Saint Yenne
**Ancestry:** Halfling
**Time Period:** ~320 A.C

A cleric who spent decades travelling the poorest regions of [[Ardia]] during the [[the Age of Darkness|Age of Darkness]], ministering to those the church had largely forgotten. Never a warrior or a martyr, she was sainted long after her death for continual, and unglamorous devotion.

#### Saint Durvan
**Ancestry:** Orc
**Time Period:** ~730 A.C

The only Orc ever sainted, Durvan served as a soldier in the [[The Holy Crusades|Third Crusade]] and is credited with holding a mountain pass alone for two days following the death of his unit, allowing his captain, and brother, to retreat safely with crucial information. Durvan did not survive, but brought enough time for his brother to escape.

#### Saint Calenne
**Ancestry:** Beastfolk (Catfolk)
**Time Period:** ~1110 A.C

A Karrethic-born cleric who came to the Faith late in life and rose to become one of the most respected theologians in the Ministry of Gospel. She wrote extensively on the relationship between the Eight Makers and magic, arguing that the Spellplague was not punishment but proof of divine mercy, as the Makers intervened to prevent extinction. Her writings remain foundational texts, though the Ministry of Prosecution has quietly disputed several of her conclusions for generations.

#### Saint Orvyn
**Ancestry:** Human
**Time Period:** ~1340 A.C

Orvyn Dass is a senior cleric of the Ministry of Gospel, currently fifty-six years old and based in the cathedral city of [[Celmia]]. He was sainted in 1341 A.C, making his canonisation one of the most recent in the Faith's history and the first of a living person in over two centuries.

The act that earned him sainthood occurred during the peak of the Great Northern Exodus. As Northerners massed at Korvia's border and the refugee camps swelled, Dass travelled north independently and spent three years ministering to the camps, teaching and learning both. He is credited with converting a significant number of Northerners to the Faith during this period, which the Faith framed as a miracle of outreach in the most forsaken of circumstances.

The sainthood sits uneasily with Orvyn himself, by most accounts. Known to be a quiet and private man who has given few public addresses since his return. During one such address, he liked being sainted while still breathing to being measured for a coffin.
