The Old Faith is a polytheistic religion formed around the gods of the [[Old Pantheon]] and the living world they govern.

### Basic Info
- **Symbol:** A Winding Pathway that leads to a Tree
- **Key Figures:** Druids, Shamans
- **Founded in:** Unknown. Believed early 50s P.C

### Background
The Old Faith is by far the oldest practiced belief system on [[Tirathen]], predating the [[Octavian Faith]] and most other religions by centuries, as well as the arrival of Humans by longer still. Where the Octavian Faith offers doctrine, hierarchy and the comforts of institutional belonging, the Old Faith offers a relationship with the world as it actually is; Chaotic, indifferent and alive.

### History
The peoples of early Tirathen lived close to the land dependent on the seasons, threatened by winters and sustained by its springs and summers. The four great elemental forces, wind, fire, earth, and water were not abstractions to them. The gods of the Old Pantheon, Auren of Wind, Soloun of Flame, Utharun of the Earth, and Lorrenil of the Ocean were understood not as distant divine patrons but as a real presences that shaped every waking hour of life. To honour them was not theology but common sense.

The earliest practitioners were those who simply had learned to read the land: a druid who knew which roots could heal a fever and which river bend would flood in spring, a shaman who could walk the boundary between the waking world and the spirit world and return to put the grieving at ease. Knowledge of the Old Faith was practical knowledge, and those who held it were valued for what they could do

Over centuries, loose networks of these practitioners formed across Valloryn. The Old Faith has never had a church, but rather a shared tradition. Symbolic language, seasonal and sacred rites that connected peoples who might otherwise have had little in common.

The winding path that leads to a great tree serves as the faith's symbol for this reason; the journey is long, but the road is shared, and so too is the destination.

#### The Spellplague
The arrival of Humans and the devastation of the Spellplague reshaped the Old Faith as with all else it touched. What survived of the Old Faith did so in the same way it had always survived; in the memories of individual practitioners, oral traditions of communities and in the sacred sites. Yet the Old Faith suffered enormously, many vital practitioners, communities and rites were lost alongside the numerous dead.

The recovery was slow and remains incomplete to the present day. Communities that had once maintained active relationships with regional practitioners found those practitioners dead or displaced, their knowledge lost. The loose networks that had connected the faith across Valloryn frayed and in many places broke entirely.

The Octavian Faith's rise in this same period was not coincidental. In the aftermath of catastrophe, the Eight Makers themselves intervened to prevent the extinction of Tirathen's native peoples. That intervention carried weight. An institution that could point to the active mercy of its gods during the worst event in living memory was an institution that could earn converts, and the Octavian Faith earned them in excess, spreading its churches, its schools, and its calendar into the recovering communities of Valloryn.

#### Suppression by advancement
The Octavian Faith has never formally declared on the Old Faith heretical or evil. No crusade has been launched against its practitioners, nor any edict of persecution issued by the Octavian Faith's many Popes. What the Faith has done, and continues to do, is quieter and more durable than violence. Wherever the Octavian Faith establishes itself in a town or village, it brings things that the Old Faith cannot offer

Sunday Schools teach children to read and write. Introducing calendars, numeracy, and record-keeping. They provide civic structure in communities that previously had none. The children of Old Faith practitioners grow up literate because of Octavian advancements, and literate people participate in an increasingly Octavian world.

The trades, the courts, the administrative functions of every major city on Valloryn operate using frameworks that the Octavian Faith helped create and continue to maintain. To be a practicing member of the Old Faith in a major city is not illegal. It is difficult, incompatible with the institutions that govern daily life.

The result is a faith that is dying not from irrelevance. Their traditions live in their elders who are unable to pass said traditions down to a generation whose frame of the divine has already been shaped by something else. Old Faith practitioners in the present day are typically disproportionately old. The knowledge they hold is being lost because there are fewer people each generation who willing to learn it.

### Core Beliefs
#### The Four as Foundation
The Old Faith holds that the four Elemental Gods of the Old Pantheon are not merely powerful beings but the fundamental forces from which the living world is made. Auren's breath moves through every living thing. Soloun's warmth makes growth possible. Utharun's body is the ground beneath every foot. Lorrenil's waters are the blood of the world itself. To understand the Old Faith is to understand that religion and environment are not separate concerns.

#### The World is Alive
The natural world is inhabited by spirits, presences that are neither gods nor mortals but something in between. A river has a spirit. A forest is filled with spirits. An ancient mountain, a particular crossroads. All living beings have a spirit. As such, all living things are understood as spiritually significant. The role of an Old Faith practitioner is partly to navigate this living world with respect, maintaining right relationships with the spirits of the places they inhabit, as well as the people and animals they interact with.

#### The Dead Return
Death in the Old Faith is not an ending but a transformation. They believe that their dead, once burned, dissolve back into the elemental world, their essence joining the great cycle of the natural order. They become part of the land, the water, the wind. This means that ancestors remain present and reachable, not as ghosts, but as presences woven into the living world. Practitioners who cultivate attentiveness to the natural world around them are, in this understanding, maintaining an ongoing relationship with those who came before.

#### The Path is Personal
Unlike the Octavian Faith, the Old Faith has no doctrines or sacred texts that adjudicates what is and is not true. Two practitioners from different regions may hold meaningfully different understandings of the same elemental god. What holds the faith together is not agreement on specifics but a shared orientation toward the natural world, the ancestors, toward the living forces that preceded civilisation and will outlast it.

### Organisation
The Old Faith currently has no organisation. At its broadest historical reach, it consisted of loose regional networks of practitioners who maintained contact through shared seasonal rites, pilgrimage to sacred sites, and the occasional gathering of druids and shamans from neighbouring communities. In the present day, those regional networks have largely collapsed.

#### Rural
What remains are isolated practitioners, most of them elderly, serving the communities that still maintain Old Faith traditions. The rural countryside of Valloryn still holds a meaningful Old Faith presence, particularly in communities far enough from major cities that Octavian infrastructure has not yet fully displaced older ways of life. The further from a city, the more likely a traveller is to find a druid tending a sacred grove, a shaman who reads the weather in the behaviour of birds, or village that marks the solstices with rites that predate the Octavian calendar by a thousand years.

#### The Frozen North
The [[Frozen North]] remains the Old Faith's most intact stronghold. The peoples of the Frozen North have had little contact with the institutions that have hollowed out the faith elsewhere. No Sunday Schools reached the tundra, nor have any Octavian cathedrals been erected in lands the rest of Valloryn have long since written off as uninhabitable. The Old Faith of the [[Free Tribes]] is not a weakened remnant of something greater. It is in its own way, the closest living approximation of what the faith looked like before it began to lose ground.

#### Druids
Druids serve as the Old Faith's primary practitioners and its closest equivalent to clergy, though they would resist that framing. A druid's authority comes from knowledge and demonstrated relationship with the living world. They tend to sacred groves, lead seasonal rites and serve as mediators between their communities and the spirits of the land. In the present day, most druids are solitary figures attached to a single community, their knowledge passed to apprentices through direct transmission.

#### Shamans
Shamans occupy a related but distinct role to Druids, particularly in the northern traditions of the Free Tribes. Where a druid's practice tends toward the living natural world, a shaman's centres on the boundary between the living and the dead. They speak to ancestors, interpret omens, and serve as guides for the dying and the grieving. Among the Free Tribes, a shaman holds a position of significant communal authority, second only in practical terms to a chieftain, and in spiritual matters, most certainly above.
