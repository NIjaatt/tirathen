[[Korvia]] is divided into seven social tiers. It is possible for individuals to rise through the hierarchal ranks of Korvian society, though it is rare.

### I. The Imperial Apex
**The Emperor**
- Is the absolute symbolic authority
- The final legitimacy for all power
- The source of titles, commands, and law

**The Two Hands of the Emperor**
- **The Chancellor** governs the Empire
- **The Marshal** commands the Empire’s wars

### II. The Imperial Blood
**The Royal Family**
- The Queen, Princes, princesses and consorts
- They are immune to most prosecution
- Treated as living symbols of the Empire

### III. The Great Nobility
**The Four Ducal Houses**
- Comprised of the Great Houses; Valcryn, Aerthain, Cassovar, Belthir
- Each Duke rules a region of Korvia outside of the capital region
- They command regional armies
- They sit in the House of Founders

### IV. The High Nobility
These are nobles without regions, but with power.

**Margraves**
- Control border marches, fortresses, or critical roads
- Often military governors
- Answer directly to a Duke or the Marshal

**Counts / Countesses**
- Rule major cities or economic hubs
- Sit in the Senate (House of Founders or Representatives depending on charter)
- Control trade, guilds, or ports

**Barons**
- Minor landholders
- Often hereditary officers or administrators
- Backbone of local governance

### V. The Lesser Nobility & Elevated Commoners
**Imperial Knights**
- Granted land, title, or privilege for service
- Often non-hereditary
- Military officers, dragon-blooded champions, or administrators

**Patricians**
- Wealthy merchant families
- Lawyers, financiers, guild masters
- Can sit in the House of Representatives

**Scholars of Standing**
- Graduates of [[Kalaen]]’s institutions
- Generals, tacticians, magistrates
- Technically common-born, socially elevated

### VI. The Commonfolk
**Freemen**
- Tradespeople, soldiers, artisans
- Protected by law (in theory)
- Can rise slightly through service

**Laborers**
- Farmers, dockhands, miners
- Little legal protection
- Viewed as resources, not citizens

### VII. Slaves
**Slaves**
- Indentured workers
- Criminal labour
- War captives
