**One Myrathin, One People**

Led by President Solen Varek.

#### Core Identity
The Union Party is the direct political descendant of the revolutionary coalition; the alliance of community leaders, reformist nobles, and working-class organizers that drafted the Myrathi Constitution. They see themselves as the custodians of the republic's founding ideals, which is both their greatest strength and their most persistent blind spot.

#### Voter Base
The Union Party draws most heavily from urban working-class Beastfolk, particularly among Catfolk and Kholo communities. They also retain significant support from Myrathin's native minority. Rural support is thinner and harder won.

#### Policy Pillars
The Union Party's platform rests on four broad commitments.

**Constitutional Protection:** The Union strongly defends and expands the rights enshrined in the Myrathi Constitution to strengthen the nations core foundations.

**Labour and Welfare:** The Union works tirelessly to uphold workers' protections, public infrastructure, social programs, and a seek a more redistributive economic model.

**Cultural Investment:** The Union is prideful, and invests in Beastfolk heritage preservation, education reform that includes accurate revolutionary history, and recognition of the seven great families' cultural significance.

**Cautious Diplomacy:** The Union's instinct is to engage Myrathin's neighbours carefully rather than antagonize them, even when those neighbours have ugly histories with Beastfolk.

#### Internal Factions
The Union Party is not monolithic, and is made up of three key sub-factions:

The Constitutionalists are Varek's wing. They are moderate, institutionalist, and committed to slow and durable reform. They believe the republic's framework, if properly maintained, is the best guarantor of Beastfolk rights long term.

The Progressives are the party's left flank, younger and more impatient. They argue that the Constitution is a compromise document built on incomplete equality and that the Constitutionalists are defending something that failed to deliver on all its promises.

Lastly, the Solidarity Caucus is the most internally controversial, especially among the Union's voter base. Their bloc sympathizes with Beastfolk nationalist grievances and tries to keep those voters inside the Union tent rather than lose them entirely to the Patriot Party.

#### Core Vulnerability
The Union Party's great weakness is that it asks Beastfolk who have every historical reason for rage to choose patience and process instead. That ask gets harder every year that tangible change feels slow.
