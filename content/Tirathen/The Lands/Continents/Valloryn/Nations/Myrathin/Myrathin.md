### History
#### Pre-Humans
[[Beastfolk]], the primary occupants of Myrathin, originally hail from the continent of [[Telmaria]], but travelled across the Abyssal Sea around 1200~1300 P.D, escaping a terrible war that was being waged in their homelands and arriving on Valloryn. Since then, they have faced horrible racism, enslavement and disgust for their bestial likeness. Yet they preferred such treatment over returning to their war-torn homelands.

The seven great families of Beastfolk as they are known today, were originally Wardens. A title denoting both military command and civic authority, drawn from old Telmarian traditions where strong families were entrusted with the protection and governance of their people during times of crisis. These Wardens supplied and guided those who sought to escape the way across the sea, to Valloryn. Eventually, the warden families too left behind Telmaria and its wars.

The seven great families are as follows; Solenne of Lions, Drevakh of Hyenas, Varath of Crocodiles, Tinn of Rats, Korryn of Ravens, Umvel of Frogs, and Greld of Boars.

The seven great families arrived on Valloryn's shores not as refugees in the common sense, but as the closest thing the beastfolk had to a governing structure. They organized the settlements, mediated disputes between the various beastfolk subspecies, and negotiated poorly, and from a position of weakness with the native Valloryn nations that despised them on sight. The early decades were brutal. Several of the seven families lost enormous influence simply trying to keep their people alive. One of which, Greld the family of boars, responded to adversity by consolidating. They controlled the best-organized beastfolk fighting forces, and quietly sold those services to outside lords who didn't care what their soldiers looked like as long as they won. It made the Grelds wealthy. It made them controversial among their own people, and it is a stain that Myrathi historians debate to this day; whether the Grelds were pragmatic survivors or collaborators who profited from their own people's desperation.

#### Formation of a Nation
About a century after the Beastfolk's arrival, Myrathin's territory had been carved out in Eastwyrd, a region the native nations had largely abandoned and considered not worth fighting over. It was the Grelds who pushed for formalization. Where the other six families remained loosely organized, governing through the old Warden tradition, Kaior Greld declared the Kingdom of Myrathin and named himself its first king.

The other six families did not all agree. Two backed the declaration, the Solenne and Varath families saw it as necessary stability. The Tinn and Korryn families protested but ultimately acquiesced. Finally, the Drevakh family refused outright, and their relationship with the Greld crown was cold and fractious for generations.

#### The Duskwall Revolution
1239 A.C saw the beginning of a revolution that would change Myrathin forever. No single cause ignited this revolution, but rather three compounding crises, all taking place one after another that broke the people's faith and trust in the monarchy:

**1. Taxes:** The King of the time, Targen IV was faced with significant debts from a failed military campaign into [[Taldar]] to reclaim the corrupted lands. So he imposed sweeping taxation reforms that fell disproportionately on the lower class, whose protests fell on deaf ears.

**2. Famine:** A catastrophic crop failure in Myrathin's west, worsened by the crown's hefty taxes and general mismanagement saw the crown prioritize grain stockpiles for the military which left thousands of citizens starving.

**3. The Eikel Massacre:** A protest of Beastfolk labourers and their native sympathizers in the city of [[Eikel]] was met by royal soldiers who drew steel on the crowd. Thirty-one dead. Nearly a hundred injured. The incident was downplayed by the crown, but word spread and quickly spiralled into the start of the revolution.

##### The Fight for Freedom
In the first six months after the massacre, the streets erupted. Spontaneous riots in the capital and major cities, burnings of tax offices and theft from grain stores. This time is what the present day remembers most vividly, and proudly recall as the people rose as one against a tyrannical force. In truth it was chaotic, leaderless, and at risk of burning itself out. The crown assumed it would.

Over the next 18 months, the military fractured. Regiments of Greld's royal guard began defecting en masse. Some noble houses, sensing the monarchy's weakness, backed republican militias for opportunistic reasons, while others stayed loyal. What followed was two years of brutal, grinding conflict across the countryside; sieges, atrocities on both sides, villages caught between loyalist and republican forces. This was a dark time in Myrathi history, and is heavily sanitized as the reality involves massacres, starvation tactics, internal republican infighting, and entire noble houses being wiped from the ledgers.

12 months later, King Targen IV was dead. Officially, he died of illness, though he was almost certainly poisoned, likely by members of his own court. With him went the crown loyalist coalition's will to continue. A council of reformist nobles, republican military commanders, and several prominent community leaders who had emerged during the conflict, negotiated the terms of a new government, a democratic republic. The Myrathi Constitution was drafted over eleven months of intense, often fractious debate, and was finalised in 1245 A.C.

The republic's constitution was founded on the ideals of Equality, Freedom and Unity, where peoples of all races and cultures would be welcomed and given the chance to live unshackled and unmarred.

#### Present Day Myrathin
Within a generation, the revolution had been smoothed into a heroic national narrative. The Eikel Massacre is remembered as the moment all of Myrathin untied and rose up as one. The ugly truth of the revolution has been entirely reframed as the crown's brutality. Though historians and scholars know the truth.

In the following years after the Myrathi Constitution was formed, a number of political parties rose and fell. Slowly formulating the present day's two party system.

### Geography
Myrathin is located in eastern Valloryn. It is bordered to the southwest by the [[Halmyr|The Holy Kingdom of Halmyr]], to the west is the [[Badlands|Badlands of Taldar]], finally to the north lies the [[Dunvaar|Dunvaar Commonwealth]].

A number of mountain ranges call Myrathin home. To the south is the Crescent Range that separates Halmyr from Myrathin. At the heart of the nation lies the Geldian Mountains, and to the east bordering with the Abyssal Ocean is the Warden Range.

Myrathin's climate is more temperate the further south you travel, but tends to be rather cold all year round given it's closeness to the Northern regions of Tirathen.

There are also a number of vast forests that Myrathin hosts, the largest of which is the Eldenwood, that lies to the west and is home to one of few [[World Tree|World Tree's]] left standing on Tirathen. The republic shares the Eldenwood with it's northern neighbour, Dunvaar.

### Government & Politics
The Republic of Myrathin operates as a presidential democracy under the framework of the Myrathi Constitution which upholds three core principles of Freedom, Equality and Unity. These tenets ensure that all citizens, regardless of race, creed, or social status, are born equal and are entitled to dignity as part of their inherent rights as living beings. Despite this, dangerous Beastfolk supremist sentiments have been growing over the last decade, and has become a persistent issue in Myrathi politics, with ethnic tensions occasionally leading to unrest across the country.

The nation's political landscape is dominated by two major parties: the minority [[Patriot Party]], led by Darra Harrian, and the majority [[Union Party]], led by President Solen Varek. There are other political parties such as the Noble Party, and the Republican Party but they lack a strong base.

**Union Party:** The Union Party is the direct political descendant of the revolutionary coalition; the alliance of community leaders, reformist nobles, and working-class organizers that drafted the Myrathi Constitution. They see themselves as the custodians of the republic's founding ideals, which is both their greatest strength and weakness.

**Patriot Party:** The Patriot Party was founded a number of decades after the revolution by veterans of the republican militias who felt that the constitution had been too generous and focused on abstract equality and not enough on rewarding those who had actually bled to build the republic.

#### International Relations
Myrathin's international relations largely depend on who is running the country at the time. Currently, under Solen Varek's Union Party, Myrathin engages their neighbours and fellow nations of Valloryn carefully, even when these nations have ugly histories with Beastfolk.

### Economy
Myrathin's economy can be divided into four main categories: mining, agriculture, and foreign trade.

#### Mining
Myrathin's various mountain ranges are home to a decent amount of natural mineral resources. Marble in particular is abundant in the Geldian mountains, and is well sought after by foreign nations.

#### Agriculture
The numerous river valleys are home to grain farms, orchards and other vegetation harvesting. Myrathin by far has the largest production of food in Valloryn.

#### Foreign Trade
Given that land routes to western Valloryn are severed by the Badlands and the [[Shifting Sands]], maritime trade is Myrathin's only option for larger international trade. Myrathin trades openly with all nations, except for Korvia who still continue the practice of Beastfolk slavery.

### Demographics
The majority of the Myrathin's populace are Beastfolk. However, the demographics of Myrathin strongly reflect the rarity seen in the [[Ancestry Overview]] document. With Dwarves, Gnomes, Goblins, Humans, Halflings, Kobolds and Orcs all being commonly seen people here.
