Karreth is a city state located in south-central [[Valloryn]]. Karreth is represented by a comet flying over a cloaked figure.

### Basic Info
- **Coat of Arms:** A Comet flying over a Cloaked Figure.
- **Demonym:** Karrethic
- **Location:** The [[Shifting Sands]]
- **Religion:** Majority worship the [[Asurion]].
- **Government:** Noocratic Magocracy

#### Formation
 + Lands believed to be first settled around ~1220 P.D
 + Officially recognised as a city state in 10 A.C

### History
#### The Arrival of Humans
At first, relations between the off-world Humans and the native peoples of Tirathen were peaceful. Humans were given permission from the Elves to remain where they arrived in [[Voryn]]. However, something else entered Tirathen alongside humans. Magic; a mysterious supernatural force that almost seems to be just as alive as any other. It spread through the lands of Tirathen, granting its boons to many. However, Magic is not native to the lands or peoples of Tirathen, their bodies not acclimated to hold such power.

[[The Spellplague]] as it became known, devasted the lands of Tirathen, but none were affected more so than the Elves, who stood on ground zero of the deadly plague. The Elves were forced to vacate their lands, and retreat to their ancestral home of [[Valeniir]], where they remained for neigh a millennia, and have only recently begun re-entering the world in the present.

#### Formation of a Nation
The first mages were Humans, and while some used their power to conquer the West and Heartlands of the new lands. Others sought refuge, away from war and chaos. They travelled east, into the Sands of [[Marqir]]. There, they founded a settlement where likeminded mages could gather and study their craft in peace.

As the natives of Tirathen began to adapt to the magic within their bodies, they found themselves treated as heretics and monsters for bearing the magic that was killing their brethren. They were driven out, or outright killed. The mages who had hidden away intervened and began to usher these outcast mages to their settlement. Over time, the settlement grew to the size of a village, then a town, and before long it was a bustling city. At its heart, Four towers stand, signifying the four spell traditions sit at the heart of the city, where mages train and study in pursuit of knowledge and power. These towers are known as [[the Tradition Towers]]. However the city officially had no name. It simply was.

But peace was not to last. One day, the sands of Marqir were infected by [[the Spellplague]], turning the desert into an unstable magical region where the sands became alive and swallowed cities whole like an ocean. The mages were quick to act, taking in refugees and magically stabilising the city. All mages of the time worked together, to cast a mighty spell that lifted the city up off the Shifting Sands below, so their city would not be swallowed whole.

As the city grew, too much work was begin placed on the four towers, distracting them from their studies. So, at the heart of the city, a central tower was constructed. A white tower, representing their government. The archmages of each tower elected one who would ascend to the white tower, leaving behind their magical ideology and governing the city with neutral wisdom.

Word spread of a city floating over the Shifting Sands of Marqir. Unable to hide themselves any longer, the city of mages revealed themselves, and named their city state; Karreth.

### Geography
Karreth is a floating city, that is magically held in place by forces unseen. The city is significantly larger than other cities in Tirathen. Fields for magical practice line the outer edges of the city, with shops, diners and taverns dotting around the city. Vast libraries and study halls span around the central region of the city. Four great towers stand around a central white tower in a quincunx formation. Between each of these towers are large lecturing halls and classrooms. The white tower, known as the [[Pinnacle]] lies at the heart of the city and is the tallest building in Karreth.

### Government & Politics
Karreth's government is a Noocratic Magocracy, where the four leading Archmages of each Tradition Tower elects their most intelligent, and wise candidates to ascend to the [[Pinnacle|Pinnacle Tower]]. Casting off their magical ideologies of their past towers, and governing Karreth as a neutral force as well as serving as diplomatic figureheads.

Mages who are selected to become Pinnacle Ascendants, are bound by a divine oath to Asurion to never cast magic during their ten year service. If they break this oath, they would invoke Asurion's wrath and be cursed with madness or even lose their magical abilities entirely. Any mage that wishes to become the Archmage of their prospective tower must take part in this service. Of the Pinnacle Ascendants, whomever is deemed the wisest and most intelligent through a series of elite tests, is elected to serve a one year term as the Prime Ascendant. The current Prime Ascendant is [[Tyrkon Halloway]].

While the Tradition Towers that surround the Pinnacle are ever present, only two have a consistently occupied by its practitioners. For Primal and Divine towers are only occupied by its tower master, as practitioners of Primal and Divine traditions find knowledge of their magic by spiritual connection and faith rather than books and study like the Arcane and Occult traditions.

#### International Relations
The Karrethic government run by Pinnacle holds a consistent, world-wide neutral stance. They hold peaceful relations with almost every country, and have no enemies. Their city is seen as true neutral ground for all countries, and has been utilized to resolve international disputes on occasion.

### Economy
Karreth has a functioning economy purely due to the strong monopoly the city state has on magical knowledge, and their consistent world-wide neutrality.

#### Contract Mages
While other nations have hedge-mages, church clerics, or bloodline prodigies, Karreth has formally trained mages that other nations happily "rent" for coin. These mages remain bound to serve Karreth first, but will follow the details of their contractors until the end date.

#### Magic Crafting
Many trained magical artisans live within Karreth, whose magical studies are focussed purely on the creation of magical items, that are sold off to high bidders.

### Demographics
The demographics of Karreth strongly reflect the rarity seen in the [[Ancestry Overview]] document. With Beastfolk, Dwarves, Gnomes, Goblins, Halflings, Kobolds and Orcs all being commonly seen people here. However, it is not uncommon to see Athamarus, Centaurs, Hobgoblins, Leshies or Minotaurs here either. Though the rare ancestries are still considered so, yet have been known to grace the city state with their presence.
