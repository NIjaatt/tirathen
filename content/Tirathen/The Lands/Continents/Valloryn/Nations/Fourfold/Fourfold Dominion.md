The Fourfold Dominion is located in central-southern [[Valloryn]]. The dominion is represented by a diamond quartered by four creatures: a Heron, a Snake, a Shark, and a Whale.

### Basic Info
- **Coat of Arms:** A diamond quartered by four creatures: a Heron, a Snake, a Shark, and a Whale.
- **Demonym:** Saltborn
- **Location:** [[Emerald Isles]]
- **Capital:** [[Wraithport]]
- **Religion:** No major religions. Most worship [[Elyna]], the Bitch Queen.
- **Government:** Thalassocratic Monarchy
+ **Pirate King:** [[Amir Al'Bahir]]

#### Formation
 + Officially recognised as a sovereign nation in 1337 P.D

### History
#### Formation of a Nation
For generations the Emerald Sea was fractured by competing pirate lords, each claiming dominion over their stretch of water. Stretching back to the days when Elyna herself roamed the seas and oceans of [[Tirathen]]. In the current [[The Age of Adventure|Age of Adventure]], the four most prominent pirate lords are [[Lohtala Searunner]], [[Qirik the Crow]], [[Sarl of the Wind]], and [[Amir Al'Bahir]].

Each of these lords felt something change in the ocean. A subtle change, that even most experienced sailors wouldn't notice, but they did. They couldn't name it, but they each felt it independently. Ships were being found destroyed and wrecked, currents shifted in ways implausible, sea creatures behaving erratically. Something was coming, a disaster of untold magnitude. When the Four Lords found that all four of them shared the same unease, that was enough.

As such, in 1335 AC the Four Lords worked together to create a nation of pirates, the Fourfold Dominion. However, issues arouse when each pirate lord wished to rule their newly founded nation by themselves. So the idea came to light to hold one last naval battle between the four lords.

#### The Emerald War
The battle between the four pirate lords went on for a year. A number of large scale skirmishes took place all across the Emerald Sea. This battle was the culmination of years of conflict and strife between the Four Lords each vying for dominance, and the coastal assaults they embarked upon after unifying under a sole banner. In 1336 A.C, the battle had ended. The sole victor, and the unanimous leader of the Fourfold Dominion was from then on Amir Al'Bahir, a Human that bore the reviled last name of the villainous Lich King, a branding of the traitors that joined hands with the Lich King some 400 years ago.

#### The Rising of the Athamaru
As Amir rose to become the Pirate King, something else rose from the Emerald Sea in 1337 A.C. The [[Athamaru]], a race of sentient fish-like people once deemed a myth and folktale, rose from the ocean seeking refuge in the lands above. Fourfold Dominion fought back against the invading Athamaru, pushing them south onto the isle of [[Nyrrath]] where the Athamaru remain to the current day.

### Geography
The Dominion has no meaningful land territory on the Valloryn mainland. Its holdings are a collection of islands and port cities scattered across the Emerald Sea. These islands serve as gathering hubs, resupply points, and centres of commerce. Some pirates never set foot on land at all, living their entire lives aboard their vessels. The Dominion considers all oceans and seas its territory, a claim no nation has yet been able to formally refute.

### Government & Politics
The Fourfold Dominion is a thalassocracy headed by the Pirate King, who holds supreme authority over all pirates and all oceans. Beneath the Pirate King serve the three Sea Lords, each commanding their own fleet and overseeing their own island territories, but sworn to the crown. The title of Sea Lord is a mark of distinction, held only by those who survived the Fourfold Battle. Rank and status throughout the Dominion are determined by three factors: personal strength and fleet size, accumulated wealth and plunder, and demonstrated loyalty to the Pirate King.

The Dominion's combined fleets form the largest and most formidable nautical army in the known world. Individual captains maintain their own ships and crews, contributing to the Dominion's collective strength when called upon. The Dominion's fleet is enormous but fluid, capable of overwhelming any naval force.

#### International Relations
Most mainland kingdoms despise the Fourfold Dominion, viewing them as savages who extort their nations while pretending at governance. However, they can do nothing to stop the Dominion's actions, as none even combined can stand against their nautical might.

### Economy
The Fourfold Dominion's economy runs on four pillars: Plunder, Taxation, Trade, and Salvage.

#### Plunder
Pirates raid coastal settlements, merchant vessels, and cargo ships, plundering goods, coin, and anything else of value. Raiding is not indiscriminate however. Experienced captains know which targets are worth the risk and which would invite retaliation too costly to weather. Raiding is celebrated culturally but practised with more calculation than outsiders assume, as certain areas are off limits to even the three Sea Lords.

#### Taxation
Any merchant vessel passing through the Emerald Sea or waters the Fourfold Dominion considers its territory is expected to pay passage. The rates are steep but not ruinous, calibrated to be cheaper than the alternative of losing an entire cargo to outright attack. Mainland kingdoms despise this arrangement deeply, viewing it as extortion dressed in the language of governance. The Fourfold Dominion views it as entirely legitimate taxation of their sovereign waters.

#### Trade
Wraithport and the Fourfold Dominion's other island ports are open to all, and a great deal of legitimate commerce flows through them. Goods that are difficult or illegal to move through mainland ports find a natural home in the Fourfold Dominion's harbours, but so does ordinary trade from merchants who find the Fourfold Dominion's lack of bureaucracy refreshing compared to dealing with mainland customs.

#### Salvage
The Emerald Sea and surrounding oceans are ancient, and their floors are littered with the wrecks of ships and civilisations that no longer exist. Salvage operations range from small crews pulling cargo from recently sunk ships to elaborate expeditions into the deep chasing rumours of lost [[Obellia|Obellian]] gold or artefacts from before the [[The Great Collapse|Great Collapse]].

### Demographics
The demographics of the confederacy strongly reflect the rarity seen in the [[Ancestry Overview]] document. With Beastfolk, Dwarves, Gnomes, Goblins, Halflings, Kobolds and Orcs all being commonly seen people here.
