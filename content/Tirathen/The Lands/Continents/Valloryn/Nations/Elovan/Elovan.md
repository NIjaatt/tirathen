Elovan is a principality located in north-central [[Valloryn]]. Valdyr is represented by a lion's head with a sword plunged into it.

### Basic Info
- **Coat of Arms:** A Lion's Head with a Sword plunged into it.
- **Demonym:** Elovanite
- **Location:** The [[Heartlands]]
- **Capital:** [[Halverin]]
- **Religion:** Majority worship the [[Octavian Faith]].
- **Government:** Protectoral Oligarchy
+ [[The Five Lords]]
 
#### Formation
 + Lands believed to be first settled around ~400 P.C

### History
#### Pre-Humans
Few know or even remember a time when the lands of the West and Heartlands were known as Erebos. A time when the lands belonged to the [[Elf|Elves]], a time before [[Human|Humans]]. Legend says that the Westlands were divided in two, each centred around a primordial god. The north revered [[Soloun]] of the Flame, whose fire kept them warm from the cold. While the south revered [[Utharun]] of the Earth, who blessed their land with abundance. These two gods each brought prosperity and peace to their people for many years, until one day, the gods went to war with each other, and so too did their people. War was raged for hundreds of years; but eventually the people realised the error in their ways, and reconciled. All while the gods continued to battle each other, and some say they are still fighting to the present.

#### The Arrival of Humans
At first, relations between the off-world Humans and the native peoples of Tirathen were peaceful. Humans were given permission from the Elves to remain where they arrived in [[Voryn]]. However, something else entered Tirathen alongside humans. Magic; a mysterious supernatural force that almost seems to be just as alive as any other. It spread through the lands of Tirathen, granting its boons to many. However, Magic is not native to the lands or peoples of Tirathen, their bodies not acclimated to hold such power.

[[The Spellplague]] as it became known, devasted the lands of Tirathen, but none were affected more so than the Elves, who stood on ground zero of the deadly plague. The Elves were forced to vacate their lands, and retreat to their ancestral home of [[Valeniir]], where they remained for neigh a millennia, and have only recently begun re-entering the world in the present.

#### Conquerors
As the natives of Tirathen were being wiped out by the Spellplague, Humans saw chance to capitalize on the weakness of their new home. As their bodies were already acclimated to magic, they used it in force to conquer, taking the lands of Korvia and Valdyr with ease. This mass expansionist war became known as [[the Hundred Year War]].

The Holy Kingdom of Halmyr responded, launching the [[The Holy Crusades|Second Holy Crusade]] in their history against the invaders of Tirathen. However, the campaign ultimately failed as their armies fell to the Spellplague, and despite having a mighty ground force, there was naught Halmyr could do in the face of the magic wielding humans.

The Humans were mighty, and were lead primarily lead by the Dragon Knight, Athelmyr and his Five Swords. Powerful warriors who served their liege with honour. However, as Humans spread further across the continent, for reasons long forgotten one of Athelmyr's swords defected to the east and claimed independence from Athelmyr.

It is for this reason, that the Hundred Year War is not remembered for the Humans mass expansion, but rather the brutal civil war between the fifth sword who held his own against the full might of Korvia.

#### [[The War of the Lions]]
The War of the Lions was a conflict between the Korvian Empire and the Kingdom of Valdyr that lasted 20 years, starting with the Valdyrian Offensive in 1320 A.C, and ending with the settlement of an uneasy peace in 1341 A.C.

By 1340, both monarchs had retreated from the front. With [[Aedric Baragav II|Aedric II]] in [[Ashkareth]], and Thalagar in [[Eryndor]]. The Crowned Sword had recently secured a political marriage between one of the high ranking families of the [[Halmyr|Holy Kingdom of Halmyr]] and his only son and heir, [[Lewys Rathven]]. Finally, his years of strengthening his ties of Halmyr's political and military might was close. Yet in one drunken night, he assaulted his future daughter-in-law and was caught in the act by his son. Lewys, outraged split from his father and defected to the east, taking his fiancé with him along with many nobles, merchants and knights. Lewys split the Kingdom of Valdyr in two, and formed the Elovanite Principality.

With both Aedric II and Thalagar facing intense political stress and turmoil back home, they simultaneously came to an agreement to cease the war in 1341 A.C.

#### Formation of a Nation
Elovan is a breakaway principality that Lewys formed in the eastern regions of the Heartlands. Though its origin lies in moral rupture rather than ambition, and despite it's recent formation, Elovan is a coherent political entity in its own right. It does not claim kingship, nor does it seek territorial expansion, yet its continued existence poses an ideological challenge to absolutist monarchy of Valdyr.

Lewys formed the government of Elovan himself, and deliberately divided his ruling power among a council of peers, all to reject Valdyr's monarchy, and to prevent the rise of one in Elovan.

To many a nations surprise at the formation of Elovan in the Heartlands, effectively stealing land from the Kingdom of Valdyr. Valdyr has yet to respond to the principality. In fact, they seem to outright ignore it's existence.

### Geography
Elovan is located in central-north Valloryn. Elovan is bordered to the west by Valdyr, and to the east by the [[Badlands]].

The Norspine Mountains shield Elovan's south from the [[Marqir|Shifting Sands of Marqir]]. Elovan's northern edge forms the coastline of the Sunken Sea.

Elovan's climate is more temperate the further south you travel, but tends to be rather cold all year round given it's closeness to the Northern regions of Tirathen.

Many mountain ranges dot the lands of Elovan. There are also a number of forests that Elovan hosts.

### Government & Politics
Elovan is a protectoral oligarchy, governed by a Lord Protector and a council of powerful noble and mercantile interests. The nation is held together by a written agreement commonly referred to as the Compact, which establishes shared sovereignty between the Lord Protector, Lewys Rathven and a council of four other ruling officials collectively known as the Five Lords.

Unlike the advisory councils found in neighbouring realms, the Five Lords possess codified authority and are not subordinate to a singular authority.

Decisions of consequence require consensus among the Five Lords, with each bringing their own competing interests to the table: martial necessity, economic sustainability, legal legitimacy, noble tradition, and urban stability.

#### International Relations
Elovan’s relationship with Valdyr is by far its most defining and sensitive relationship. It is a strained relationship, built upon anger and resentment. Yet neither nation openly antagonise the other, and in fact have open communication with each other. However, they avoid direct dealings and cooperation with each other.

Elovan views Korvia as the most capable external threat to the stability of their nation, and with the most cause.

Elovan holds a positive relationship with most other countries of Valloryn.

### Economy
Elovan's economy can be divided into four main categories: agriculture, forestry, minerals and foreign trade.

#### Agriculture
The region of [[Dornach]] consists of vast open plains upon which grain is farmed. Livestock are herded and goods processed.

#### Forestry
The region of [[Skeld]] is mostly comprised of single large forest. The timber there is strong, and old, prized for its strength and flexibility.

#### Minerals
The Norspine Mountains to the south are rich with many rare and beautiful minerals and metals.

#### Foreign Trade
A number of merchants across the lands invest in Elovan, seeking their luxury goods of minerals, timber, fine wool, leathers, furs and much more. Elovan's markets are also permissive of foreign coin, making it a hub for exchange.

### Demographics
The majority of the Elovanite populace are Humans. However, the demographics of Elovan strongly reflect the rarity seen in the [[Ancestry Overview]] document. With Beastfolk, Dwarves, Gnomes, Goblins, Halflings, Kobolds and Orcs all being commonly seen people here.
