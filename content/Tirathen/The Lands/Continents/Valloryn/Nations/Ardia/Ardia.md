### History
#### Pre-Humans
In the early days of Ardia, the lands were occupied by [[Goblin|Goblins]], who created many settlements and worshipped many gods of their own creation now considered pagan. Many scholars believe Goblins were the original settlers of [[Marqir]], but traversed the Red Mountains in pursuit of fertile lands. Little is known about the lives of Goblins in this era, but what is known is at some point, something threatened their lives enough to for them to begin experimenting with themselves, seeking to enhance their bodies. It is believed this threat was likely one of [[the Dooms of Tirathen]], though it is unknown which one specifically.

#### Evolution
As a result of this experimentation, a new genetic creature was created. The [[Hobgoblin]], taller and stronger than their creators, they were built for dominating in all matters of war and conflict. Whether or not Hobgoblins fulfilled their intended purpose, no-one knows.

However, the experimentation was far from over. Many goblins continued to alter and enhance select Hobgoblins, until they created what they believed to be the pinnacle of strength and might; the [[Orc]]. However their ultimate creation turned on them, as Orcs seized control of Ardia, and ruled over the lands.

#### The Arrival of Humans
Ardia, being cut off from the rest of the world, were unbothered by the arrival of Humans. That was until the consequences of their arrival came. [[The Spellplague]] as it became known, devasted the lands of Tirathen. Many Orcs, Hobgoblins and Goblins perished to the spellplague, but those who survived were stronger for it. Something Ardian society relished in.

#### The Lich King
In 618 A.C, an immensely powerful being was reborn as a Lich, and named himself the Lich King. Tyr Al'Bahir, as he was known awoke in the lands of Ardia, where he had been buried nearly a thousand years ago. Many Ardians were fascinated by the Lich King's strength, and willingly followed him as Tyr gathered allies both alive and undead on his journeys across Valloryn. Soon, Tyr and his armies had conquered almost all of [[Korvia]] and brought its people under Tyr al'Bahir's control. That was when the [[The Holy Crusades|Third Crusade]] began, which fractured Ardia between those that followed Tyr, and those that did not. After nearly 50 years of war, Tyr was defeated and his soul sealed within his fortress, [[Darkspire]] within the lands of Ardia. Tyr would be unable to rise again, as long as the seal remained in tact.

#### Consequences
After Tyr's defeat, Ardia was fractured and ruined by war, being the location of Tyr's last stand. The [[Halmyr|Holy Kingdom of Halmyr]] would not risk the potential destruction of the Lich King's seal, and thus had their crusaders occupy and dismantle Ardia's Orcish government at the time. The instated their own ruling body, comprised of Halmyran nobles and merchants. Utilizing the knowledge of a few "traitorous" Orcs, Goblins and Hobgoblins, Halmyr banned uncontracted militias, and settlements. Those who refused to follow the new colonised Ardia were encouraged to leave, as long as they did not unite against Halmyran rule, or settle near Darkspire. 

Many Goblins folded into the new Halmyran government, and found stable work as clerks, engineers, and alchemists. Meanwhile Hobgoblins are rather divided on colonial rule. Many serve in Ardia's new army, but some prefer joining their Orc cousins.

Orcs, being the rulers of Old Ardia were by far the most negatively affected by Halmyr's colonisation. Many orcs left Ardia, and found stable work as mercenaries, and quickly became famous for their strength, and honest approach to contract work.

In the present day, Ardia is a ghost nation, colonised by Halmyr to ensure the protection of Darkspire's seal. The nation is funded by Halmyr, and has a strong tourist economy within its capital city. Meanwhile the rest of Ardia's inhabitants that rejected Halmyr's rule are fragmented and broken.

### Geography
Ardia is located in southern Valloryn. To it's north, lies the [[Shifting Sands]] of Marqir.

The Red Mountains shield Ardia's north from the Shifting Sands. Ardia is bordered to the south, west and east by the Emerald Sea.

Ardia's climate is generally temperate but is very humid along the coastline.

Many hills and small mountain ranges dot the lands of Ardia. There are also a number of forests that Ardia hosts.

### Government & Politics
The Ardian Protectorate is a colonial theocratic state, governed by Halmyr in the name of protection, and maintained to contain the threat of Tyr Al'Bahir. Ardia is governed as a problem to be managed. Halmyr has not replicated their full religious theocracy in Ardia, as that would require faith, proximity and trust of the citizens. None of which are easily achievable due to how Halmyr colonised Ardia. Halmyr has three core pillars of governance over Ardia; the High Protector, the Office of Judicial Control, and the Containment Forces.

**The High Protector:** The High Protector of Ardia is the single, most visible ruler of the Protectorate. They are appointed directly by the Pope of the Octavian Faith, and are usually a senior cleric-noble or high ranking veteran of one of the Faith's ministries. A new High Protector is brought in roughly every 10-20 years to limit prolonged exposure to what the Faith believes are cursed lands. The High Protector mandates a number of roles; they maintain political stability of Ardia, enforce Halmyran law and doctrine, prevent the rise of any native powers, and serve as Halmyr's voice and hands for the nation of Ardia. The High Protector governs over Ardia from the coastal fort of Kraggath.

**The Office of Judicial Control:** The Office of Judicial Control is the colonial extension of the Octavian Faith's Ministry of Prosecution, with expanded authority. They play a major part in controlling the populace of Ardia. They have absolution jurisdiction over investigations into; heresy, sedition, anti-Halmyran activity and rhetoric, unauthorized Orcish assemblies. They have the authority to override local native courts and rulership, as well as the power to detain citizens indefinitely "for doctrinal review".

**The Containment Forces:** The Containment Forces are a faction of Halmyr's armies. They maintain a permanent presence in Ardia and are tasked specifically with the guarding of Darkspire, the enforcement of restricted zones and areas, as well as the suppression of uprisings. The Containment Forces are comprised of not only Halmyr's Knights, but also Hobgoblins and Orcs that have converted.

#### International Relations
Nations view Ardia as a vassal state of Halmyr, and thus rarely outright do business or foster a relationship with them, instead favouring Halmyr. To give credit, Halmyr does what it can to bolster recognition and foster international relations.

### Economy
Ardia's economy can be divided into three main categories: Mercenary Corps, Maritime Hubs, Minerals.

#### Mercenary Corps.
By far, Ardia's most valuable export is not a resource. It's the Orcish Mercenary Corps. Orcs are famed for their contractual honesty, endurance and strength, as well as their intense discipline. Such aspects make them highly sought after continent-wide.

#### Maritime Hubs
Ardia's coastline feature a number of port towns and cities that serve as hubs for trade. Merchants gather here, as well as pirates and all manner of coastal folk looking to make coin.

#### Minerals
Lastly, Ardia's Red Mountains don't just protect the Southlands from the Shifting Sands. The mountains are rich with Iron, minerals and jewels, as well as a rare and beautiful red-marble that is highly sought after by nobles across the continent.

### Demographics
The majority of the Ardian populace are Orcs. However, the demographics of Ardia strongly reflect the rarity seen in the [[Ancestry Overview]] document. With Dwarves, Gnomes, Goblins, Humans and Halflings, all being commonly seen people here. As well as the more uncommon ancestries such as Athamaru and Hobgoblins.
