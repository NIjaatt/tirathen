The Dunvaar Commonwealth is nation located in far north-eastern [[Valloryn]]. Dunvaar is represented by a Gauntlet holding a Gear Cog.

### Basic Info
**Coat of Arms:** A Gauntlet holding a Gear Cog.

**Location:** [[Iron North]]

### History
#### Pre-Humans
Long before the birth of the Dunvaar Commonwealth, the Dwarves and Gnomes of the Iron North were already a people intertwined. As neighbours, they shared trade, crafting traditions, and a mutual respect for the value of hard work and ingenuity. Their nations and people had little ambition beyond the perfection of their craft. The Iron North was theirs, quiet and industrious, and they wanted nothing more.

When the Dooms of Tirathen awoke and set upon the world, few peoples suffered as swiftly or as completely as the Iron Northers. As the Tarrasque, First Doom and Father of Monsters, carved a path of annihilation through their lands. Facing extinction, the survivors fled to the only place they could. They retreated into the mines and tunnels they had spent generations digging, and sealed themselves beneath the earth and waited. But something unexpected waited for them in the deep.

Beyond the familiar tunnels of their old mines, the survivors broke through into a world they had never imagined. Vast natural caverns stretched for leagues in every direction. These caverns possessed their own ecosystems, strange fungi and creatures that had never known sunlight. Rivers of clean water carving through ancient stone, and veins of mineral wealth so rich the survivors, now founders wept.

When the Age of Calamity finally ended and the Tarrasque was driven back by the rising heroes of Tirathen, the founders did not return to the surface. Not when everything worth having was already within their grasp.

#### Formation of a Nation
The centuries that followed were ones of quiet transformation. Freed from the entanglements of the surface world and sheltered beneath the earth, the founders turned their full attention to building their future. The caverns were mapped, expanded, and connected. The first settlements becoming cities referred to as Holds.

Recognising that neither the Dwarves nor the Gnomes could claim sole credit for what they had built together, and that neither people would accept subordination to the other, a dual leadership was formed to lead their Commonwealth. One Dwarven ruler, and one Gnomish ruler, each selected by merits. The greatest mind and the greatest craftsperson that their respective peoples could produce. This was the founding principle of what would become their conjoined meritocracy and technocracy, the idea that the only qualification for a leader was knowledge and experience. Thus was born, the Dunvaar Commonwealth.

#### The Spellplague
The arrival of Humans and the spread of the Spellplague did not spare the Dunvaar Commonwealth simply because they lived underground. Magic was not a surface phenomenon. It was a new force woven into the very fabric of Tirathen itself, and it reached into the deep just as readily as it spread across the plains of Valloryn. The Commonwealth lost a significant portion of its population in the years that followed, as bodies unaccustomed to the presence of magic failed and perished.

#### Firepower
When the world entered the Age of Darkness, Dunvaar was unbothered by the half millennia of sunless sky. Already accustomed to the dark. It was in this long, uninterrupted period of isolation that their greatest technological leaps were made. Gunpowder was discovered, refined, and even weaponised. Steam engines were developed and fitted into great machines to move resources and connect the great holds of the Commonwealth. By the time the sun returned and the current era began, the Dunvaar Commonwealth had become something entirely unlike any other nation on Tirathen. Yet their advancements have remained a secret to all but a few.

### Geography
Dunvaar is located in north-eastern Valloryn. It is bordered to the southwest by the [[Badlands|Badlands of Taldar]], and to the southeast is [[Myrathin|the Republic of Myrathin]].

Dunvaar is home to a massive mountain range called the Grey Mountains, in the Common tongue. It spans and covers the vast majority of their surface lands.

Dunvaar is cold all year round, and snows often. The north-east of Dunvaar in particular is extremely cold, as vast ice glaciers form of the coast.

There are two main forests that call Dunvaar home. There is the Vurthren Forest, and also the Eldenwood which the Commonwealth shares with its neighbour, Myrathin.

### Government & Politics
The Dunvaar Commonwealth operates as a meritocratic technocracy, governed jointly by two elected rulers, a Dwarven Jovr and a Gnomish Thegin, who must reach consensus on all matters of national importance. Beneath them sits the Faction Council, composed of the leaders of Dunvaar's crafting factions, each representing a distinct discipline of industry and invention. The factions govern their own affairs independently, and it is through them that the daily life of every Dunvaaran is shaped and organised.

**Dual Rulership:** At the head of the Dunvaar Commonwealth stand two rulers, the Dwarven Jovr (Yo-Ver) and the Gnomish Thegin (They-gen). These rulers are each selected through the Trial of Works, a rigorous merit-based competition in which any citizen of Dunvaar may produce a singular masterwork that is then judged by the Faction Council. To become a ruler, the masterwork must demonstrate not only technical mastery but vision and advancement of Dunvaaran craft. No ruler inherits their seat, and none may claim it through political manoeuvring alone. The work speaks, or it does not. The Jovr and Thegin must reach consensus on all matters of national importance, a deliberate design that ensures neither people holds unilateral authority over the Commonwealth.

Currently, the Dual rulers of Dunvaar are Haldrum Brakfeld the Jovr, a veteran smith and engineer, whose tenure has been defined by the expansion of the Iron Rail network. Beside him sits the Thegin, Peldrivane Osselwick known to most simply as Pel, a Gnomish mechanist whose Trial of Works produced a steam regulation system that doubled the efficiency of every engine in the Commonwealth overnight. Both are mid-tenure, established figures whose working relationship is regarded as one of the more harmonious pairings the Commonwealth has seen in living memory.

**The Faction Council:** Beneath the Jovr and Thegin sits the Faction Council, the body through which the daily life of every Dunvaaran is governed. The Council is composed of the elected heads of each of Dunvaar's crafting factions, of which there are many, each representing a distinct discipline of industry and invention. Factions govern their own affairs independently, managing resources, setting craft standards, and resolving internal disputes without interference from the dual rulers. Of the numerous factions, three are dominant political forces above all others, the [[Stonebloods]], the [[Sparkwelds]], and the [[Ironrunners]].

#### International Relations
Dunvaar famously is a truly neutral nation. They care not for the ongoings of other nations and people. Their focus is purely focused inward.

Other nations hold Dunvaar in high regard for their impeccable craftsman, engineers and inventors. Weapons, Armour and Items that are exported from Dunvaar are well sought after and always sell for a high price.

Other nations try their best to be amicable towards Dunvaar, as their leaders are aware of the true capabilities of Dunvaar could be if they ever turned hostile.

### Economy
Dunvaar's economy is built almost entirely on exports, and it is one of the most enviable economies on the continent of Valloryn as a result. Sitting upon mineral wealth that most surface nations could not imagine, and worked by craftsman whose skill is without equal, Dunvaar produces goods that the rest of the world simply cannot replicate.

#### Crafts
Weapons and armour bearing the mark of a Dunvaaran smith command extraordinary prices across Valloryn. Their works are sought by all. Kings, mercenary companies, and private collectors alike.

#### Materials
Rare metals, and gemstones pulled from the earth as well as refined alloys also represent another pillar of Dunvaar's exports.

#### Foreign Trade
To trade with Dunvaar is considered a mark of prestige among merchants and nations alike, and competition for Dunvaaran goods is a persistent fight among the merchants of Valloryn.

Dunvaar will trade any of their completed works without issue, except for three items. Dunvaar has banned the sale of their gunpowder, firearms and steam engines. The commonwealth understands the dangers these technologies could have in the wrong hands. No offer of coin, political alliance or diplomatic pressure has ever moved them on this point.

### Demographics
The majority of the Dunvaar's populace are Dwarves, Gnomes and Halflings. All other ancestries seen in the [[Ancestry Overview]] document are considered uncommon or rare.
