### History
#### Pre-Humans
Originally, the Elves were among the most widely settled peoples of Valloryn. Their homeland in the continental interior was a vast territory they called Erebos, which broadly corresponds to what is now [[Korvia]] and [[Valdyr]]. They were not an empire in the Human sense. They had no standing armies of conquest, no tribute-extracting bureaucracy. They had no need of such things, as they were simply culturally dominant. Their *aiudara gates* made them extraordinarily well-connected across the continent, allowing for near instant teleportation. Valeniir existed during this period as their ancestral heartland, a place of deep significance, but it was not a refuge. It was a home among other homes.

#### The Spellplague
When Humans first arrived through the portal in western Erebos in 1202 P.D, the Elves were cautious but not hostile. They permitted Human settlement in a designated region of Erebos, what is now [[Voryn]]. They operated under the assumption that the newcomers could be managed. This proved to be a catastrophic miscalculation.

Magic arrived with the Humans, and it killed indiscriminately. As the Spellplague tore through the native peoples of Tirathen, the Elves suffered enormously. Their oracles, forewarned of the coming devastation, urged retreat. The Humans, their bodies already acclimated to magic, pressed the advantage, and the Elves weakened and grieving could do nothing as Erebos was consumed.

They cast off their pride, and withdrew to Valeniir. The Elves sealed what elf gates they could on the mainland, and collapsed others behind them. Once safe on Valeniir, they raised the coastal wards that remain in place to this day.

When [[the Great Collapse]] followed in 1508 P.D and the Age of Darkness descended, they were already behind their walls, safe and prepared. For the centuries that followed, Valeniir endured. Isolated, grieving, and deeply changed by what had been lost, but intact.

#### The Long Mourning
The period between the Spellplague and the present day is what Elves call the Long Mourning. Nearly eight hundred years of near-total withdrawal from the affairs of Valloryn. From the outside, Elves slowly dwindled into myth and legend. However, they were anything but idle. Valeniir spent this time consolidating their people, rebuilding their populace, mourning the lost, and arguing. While the outside world saw a unified wall of silence, inside there were always those who wanted to know what was happening beyond it.

However, eight hundred years; the entirety of the Long Mourning, represents perhaps one or two generations of Elven life. The Elves alive today who sit on the High Council are the  children of those who experienced and survived the Spellplague. They've heard stories of Erebos. They are not a people who have had time to forget and move on.

### Geography
Valeniir is an island located off the south-eastern coast of Valloryn, separated from the mainland by ocean and seas.

The island itself is heavily forested, ancient woodland covering much of its interior. Valeniir's climate ranges from tropical to temperate. A vast mountain range runs along western side of the island. Valeniir is also home to the six [[World Tree|World Tree's]], the most of any other region in [[Tirathen]].

The coastline is effectively impassable to outsiders. The Elves maintain a network of magical wards that extend outward from the shore, creating an exclusion that has held for over a millennia.

### Government & Politics
Valeniir is governed by a High Council, comprised of the five oldest noble houses in Elven society. Each house holds one seat on the Council, and decisions are reached by majority vote. The five houses have shifted in influence and alignment across the centuries, and the present day is no exception. The council's present alignment is defined by a fault line running through the Council: isolationism versus re-engagement.

#### The Fault Line
The three-to-two split on the Council is not merely a political disagreement. At its root, it is a generational one.

Houses Sylvar and Aerindal were alive when the Spellplague happened and survived it. They remember Erebos. They watched it become Korvia. The devastation that followed is not history to them, but lived experience, carried across eight centuries that have done nothing to diminish its weight. Their isolationism is not stubbornness. It is the only position that feels honest to people who were actually there.

Meanwhile, Houses Venthryn, Thessilorn, and Myarthyn know the Spellplague as their elders know it. Thoroughly and in detail, with full respect for its horror. But they know it as history. 

The gap between those two ways of knowing, is where the fault line runs and it is not a gap that argument can easily close.

#### International Relations
To Valloryn's nations, Valeniir is a mystery that has never been worth the cost of solving. The island's magical wards are known by reputation, and the reputation is sufficient. Most nations maintain a nominal diplomatic acknowledgment of Valeniir's existence without any functional relationship.

Meanwhile, toward the Council views their sovereign nations of Dwarves, Gnomes, and Beastfolk: Valeniir holds no particular issue with them. These peoples were not responsible for the fall of Erebos. Relations, where they exist at all, are distant but not hostile.

However, the Council's official posture toward Human-led nations is cold indifference. Among older Elves, a wound exists that has not fully healed. The Humans who drove them from Erebos are not the Humans alive today, and Elves are too rational to conflate them entirely. But rationality and grief do not always occupy the same space.

### Economy
Valeniir's economy is entirely self-sufficient. No foreign trade, no external dependency, no material gap that outside contact is needed to fill. What Valeniir cannot grow, harvest, or make for itself, it has learned to do without. There is no merchant class in the traditional sense, no import trade, and no port culture. Valeniir's internal market has been built using three pillars.

#### Land
The lands of Valeniir are extraordinarily fertile, its ancient forests and climate are key for producing a diverse agricultural yield that sustains the population with room to spare. Elven husbandry is unhurried and meticulous; crops are tended across timescales the shorter-lived peoples would find impractical, vineyards cultivated for decades before their first harvest is considered worth even smelling.

Beyond agriculture, the island's natural resources feed directly into Valeniir's craft economy. Rare timber from the ancient interior forests, mineral deposits along the island's western range, and a variety of endemic herbs and botanicals found nowhere else on Tirathen form the raw material base from which everything else is built. A popular saying in Valeniir is "Véra a’Lúin", which means "The land provides"

#### Craft
House Myarthyn's domain, and Valeniir's most developed economic pillar. The artisanal tradition of Valeniir is quite frankly, the product of a civilisation that has had nothing to do for nearly a millennia but refine its craft to such a degree of perfection that the outside world has never seen.

Elven textiles, metalwork, perfumery, preserved botanicals, and decorative art are produced at a such a high quality Valeniir that they would command extraordinary prices in any market on Valloryn; if they were available.

Craft production functions as the primary currency of social prestige and class. A house that produces renowned artisans carries weight in the social hierarchy. A family whose work is collected and celebrated accumulates influence that translates directly into social and political standing. The economy and the social hierarchy are not separate systems, but one in the same.

#### Arcanism
Valeniir's arcane tradition is among the oldest continuous schools of magical study on Tirathen, and the centuries of isolation have allowed it to develop along entirely its own lines, uninfluenced by the mages of of the mainland. Elven magic as practised in Valeniir looks, to outside eyes, nothing like what is taught or demonstrated in [[Karreth]], yet the end goals of their spells remain the same.

This scholarship is not purely academic. Magical services; enchantment, warding, healing, the maintenance of the coastal ward network itself. All function as a form of internal currency. The houses that produce the most accomplished mages hold strong leverage over the infostructure of the nation. House Aerindal's arcane scholars, whatever their political views, are indispensable to the functioning of the wards that keep Valeniir safe.

### Demographics
The majority of the Valeniir's populace are Elves. All other ancestries seen in the [[Ancestry Overview]] document are considered rare except for Halflings which are uncommon.
