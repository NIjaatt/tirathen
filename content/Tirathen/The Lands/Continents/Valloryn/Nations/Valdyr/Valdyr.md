Valdyr is a kingdom located in north-central [[Valloryn]]. Valdyr is represented by a rearing Lion.

### Basic Info
**Coat of Arms:** A Rearing Lion
**Demonym:** Valdyrian
**Location:** The [[Heartlands]]
**Capital:** [[Eryndor]]
**Religion:** Majority worship the [[Octavian Faith]].
**Government:** Absolutist Monarchy
 - **Crowned Sword:** [[Thalagar Rathven]]
 - **Advisory Council:** [[The Five Voices]]
 **Formation:** 
 + Believed to be first settled around ~400 P.C
 + Officially recognised as a sovereign nation in 1400 P.D

### History
#### Pre-Humans
Few know or even remember a time when the lands of the West and Heartlands were known as Erebos. A time when the lands belonged to the [[Elf|Elves]], a time before [[Human|Humans]]. Legend says that the Westlands were divided in two, each centred around a primordial god. The north revered [[Soloun]] of the Flame, whose fire kept them warm from the cold. While the south revered [[Utharun]] of the Earth, who blessed their land with abundance. These two gods each brought prosperity and peace to their people for many years, until one day, the gods went to war with each other, and so too did their people. War was raged for hundreds of years; but eventually the people realised the error in their ways, and reconciled. All while the gods continued to battle each other, and some say they are still fighting to the present.

#### The Arrival of Humans
At first, relations between the off-world Humans and the native peoples of Tirathen were peaceful. Humans were given permission from the Elves to remain where they arrived in [[Voryn]]. However, something else entered Tirathen alongside humans. Magic; a mysterious supernatural force that almost seems to be just as alive as any other. It spread through the lands of Tirathen, granting its boons to many. However, Magic is not native to the lands or peoples of Tirathen, their bodies not acclimated to hold such power.

[[The Spellplague]] as it became known, devasted the lands of Tirathen, but none were affected more so than the Elves, who stood on ground zero of the deadly plague. The Elves were forced to vacate their lands, and retreat to their ancestral home of [[Valeniir]], where they remained for neigh a millennia, and have only recently begun re-entering the world in the present.

#### Conquerors
As the natives of Tirathen were being wiped out by the Spellplague, Humans saw chance to capitalize on the weakness of their new home. As their bodies were already acclimated to magic, they used it in force to conquer, taking the lands of Korvia and Valdyr with ease. This mass expansionist war became known as [[the Hundred Year War]].

The Holy Kingdom of Halmyr responded, launching the [[The Holy Crusades|Second Holy Crusade]] in their history against the invaders of Tirathen. However, the campaign ultimately failed as their armies fell to the Spellplague, and despite having a mighty ground force, there was naught Halmyr could do in the face of the magic wielding humans.

The Humans were mighty, and were lead primarily lead by the Dragon Knight, Athelmyr and his Five Swords. Powerful warriors who served their liege with honour. However, as Humans spread further across the continent, for reasons long forgotten one of Athelmyr's swords defected to the east and claimed independence from Athelmyr.

It is for this reason, that the Hundred Year War is not remembered for the Humans mass expansion, but rather the brutal civil war between the fifth sword who held his own against the full might of Korvia.

#### Formation of a Nation
After decades of relentless conflict, the fifth sword, who Valdyr remembers as Alaric Rathven, had broken free of Athelmyr as the Dragon Knight and his armies finally ceded their assaults. By the end of the war, he had unified scattered warbands, once loyal Korvian knights, and the displaced peoples Korvia under his personal banner.

Villages and towns coalesced around fortified keeps, forming large settlements, and each community swore fealty directly to Alaric rather than to intermediaries. Alaric was named king by those that followed him, and he named the lands they had severed from Korvia, Valdyr, but he rejected the idea of kingship. That was, until Korvia mockingly began calling him the Crowned Sword. A title that he took upon himself and his line in defiance.

Upon Alaric's death, his legacy passed to his heirs. His eldest son established the first enduring institutions of Valdyr, including the council of the Five Voices, each charged with advising the Crowned Sword on matters of war, coin, magic, people, and faith. Rather than devolving power to semi-autonomous nobles, this system centralized authority with the Crowned Sword while ensuring that the burdens of governance and war were shared among capable experts.

Over generations, this framework solidified into a durable kingdom. Tight, efficient, and ever vigilant. The people of Valdyr see their nation as forged by steel, honour, and the memory of the Alaric's defiance of Korvia.

#### [[The War of the Lions]]
The War of the Lions was a conflict between the Korvian Empire and the Kingdom of Valdyr that lasted 20 years, starting with the Valdyrian Offensive in 1320 A.C, and ending with the settlement of an uneasy peace in 1341 A.C.

In 1340, both monarchs had retreated from the front. With [[Aedric Baragav II|Aedric II]] in [[Ashkareth]], and Thalagar in [[Eryndor]]. The Crowned Sword had recently secured a political marriage between one of the high ranking families of the Holy Kingdom of Halmyr and his only son and heir, [[Lewys Rathven|Lewys]]. Finally, his years of strengthening his ties of Halmyr's political and military might was close. Yet in one drunken night, he assaulted his future daughter-in-law and was caught in the act by his son. Lewys, outraged split from his father and defected to the east, taking his fiancé with him along with many nobles, merchants and knights. Lewys split the Kingdom of Valdyr in two, and formed the Elovanite Principality.

With both Aedric II and Thalagar facing intense political stress and turmoil back home, they simultaneously came to an agreement to cease the war in 1341 A.C.

### Geography
Valdyr is located in central-north Valloryn. Valdyr is bordered to the east by [[Elovan]], and to the west by [[Korvia]].

The great Westwall Range runs along the western edge of Valdyr's lands, shielding the land from Korvia, except for a single pass. Similarly, the Norspine Mountains shield Valdyr's south from the [[Marqir|Shifting Sands of Marqir]]. Valdyr's northern edge forms the coastline of the North Sea.

Valdyr's climate is more temperate the further south you travel, but tends to be rather cold all year round given it's closeness to the Northern regions of Tirathen.

Many mountain ranges dot the lands of Valdyr, especially in the western regions. There are also a number of forests that Valdyr hosts.

### Government & Politics
Valdyr's government functions as an absolutist monarchy. The Crowned Sword alone holds absolute executive, legislative and judicial authority. Since the beginning of Valdyr, the Crowned Sword has had a council of competent advisors to influence and aid the Crowned Sword's decision making. This council is [[The Five Voices]], who act and command the people of Valdyr on behalf of their Crowned Sword.

**The Crowned Sword:** The Crowned Sword is essentially the King of Valdyr and the highest authority in Valdyr. Their legitimacy is defined by martial prowess, as are all positions of potential power within Valdyr. The ruling family of Valdyr, the Rathven's, reside at the nation's capital, Eryndor. The current Crowned Sword, Thalagar Rathven lives there alone, his wife deceased, and his only son and heir, Prince Lewys Rathven having fled to the east, and forming the [[Elovan|Elovanite Principality]] due to the current Crowned Sword's actions.

**The Five Voices:** The Five Voices consists of five highly competent advisors who advise the Crowned Sword on matters of the realm. They cover war, the economy, the arcane, internal stability and faith. They give their advise, and the Crowned Sword makes a decision, which the Five Voices then direct and command the people of Valdyr on their behalf.

#### International Relations
Valdyr has little concern for countries outside of Korvia, aside from the [[Halmyr|Holy Kingdom of Halmyr]], which is the seat of their primary faith.

Famously, Valdyr and Korvia are frequently at war with one another fuelled by an ancient, long held grudge. Despite their propagandized image of a war hungry nation, Valdyr holds peaceful relations with most other countries, especially [[Karreth]].

Finally, Valdyr has a strained relationship with the Elovanite Principality. Despite it being their land, the Crowned Sword doesn't seek to retake the lands. Instead, views their ruling lord, and his only son Lewys with guilt. Valdyr is ready to defend itself from any potential attacks from the east, but would never retaliate.

### Economy
Valdyr's economy can be divided into three main categories: mining, agriculture, and defensive pacts.

#### Mining
The Norspine Mountains to the south are rich with many rare and beautiful minerals and metals.

#### Agriculture
Valdyr plays host to some of Valloryn's largest farmlands, all used to supply the people and Valdyrian armies.

#### Defence Pacts
The most important products for export are Valdyr's strength. Since the War of the Lions, Valdyr has made several defensive pacts with other countries in exchange for economic investment. Their military might, being a compelling reason to form such an agreement.

### Demographics
The majority of the Valdyrian populace are Humans. However, the demographics of Valdyr strongly reflect the rarity seen in the [[Ancestry Overview]] document. With Beastfolk, Dwarves, Gnomes, Goblins, Halflings, Kobolds and Orcs all being commonly seen people here.
