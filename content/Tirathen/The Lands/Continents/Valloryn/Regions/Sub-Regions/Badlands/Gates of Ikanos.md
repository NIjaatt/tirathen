The gates of Ikanos refer to a number of giant wardstones that line the border of [[Taldar]], and are named after the Pope that saw to their creation, Pope Ikanos.

### Setup
Even though warriors had been traveling to Taldar to battle the demonic hordes ever since the Darkrift opened in 945 P.D, the First Crusade was not named so by the Holy Kingdom until 961 P.D. Its "late" start can be attributed to the general environmental and social upheaval caused by the mass appearance of Demons worldwide.

In 945 P.D, [[The Darkrift]] opened in the lands of Taldar. A year later, when the crusaders of [[Halmyr]] arrived, the discovered that the Kingdom of Taldar had fallen, and parts of western [[Myrathin]] had already been overrun. The nations of [[Dunvaar]], [[Valdyr]], and Myrathin, each immediately threatened by the demonic invasion, joined the Holy Kingdom in their efforts.

Their numbers and organizational strengths added greatly to the defending armies, and soon the demonic forces began to be pushed back into Taldar. Having thus liberated all of the surrounding regions outside of Taldar, they attempted to push further into Taldar only to be met with fierce resistance.

### The Creation of the Gates
After five years of attempting to push into Taldar, Pope of the era, Ikanos Helovain decreed that Taldar and the demons would be sealed. Great wardstones were forged by hand of the Dwarves of Dunvaar, carved with runes by the Elves of Valdyr, and imbued with Halmyr's divine powers. These great wardstones were placed all along the borders of Taldar, each one connected to it's neighbouring stones. Creating a barrier that kept the demonic forces from passing. These wardstones would come to be known as the Gates of Ikanos.
