The Age of Creation refers to the uncounted years of mythic time, beginning with the creation of Tirathen and ending with the emergence of mortal beings upon its face (the Age of Dragons). It was a time when the deities of Tirathen played a much more active role than they do today. It was during the Age of Creation that the great battle that led to [[Nhalgorath|Nhalgorath's]] imprisonment occurred.

### In the Beginning...
In the beginning, there were concepts. Great beings the encompassed a particular concept, which dictated their strengths and weaknesses. True monsters. The most powerful concept among them, and their King, was Death. The only concept that could kill and absorb another concept. After untold eons of time, the concepts banded together with a plan. They would all be absorbed by Death, in order to create something new. So Death did as Death does. It killed and consumed each and every concept, taking into itself. Until it was alone.

Death gathered all the concepts it had eaten and condensed them into a seed, and hurled it into the cosmic expanse, and Death waited.

Death, was now alone. It felt nothing. It was nothing, and it was sorrowful. Until suddenly, Death was no longer alone. Yggdrasil, the world tree, had begun to sprout from the seed. Death was awe-struck by Yggdrasil and became its greatest guardian. But Death foresaw the fickle and chaotic nature of Yggdrasil, and by extension, life. Death knew it would someday grow and twist in harmful ways, so, for the good of all, Death took his place as most know of him today. Not as a person, or even a deity, but as an inevitability. All things that begin must also end.

### The Fundamental Gods
Time unknown passed as Yggdrasil grew and flourished. Eventually, creating a race of beings that could forge their own worlds under her branches. These beings, were the first [[Gods|Deities]]. These deities banded together into various groups and set off in search of the perfect place to forge their worlds. One such group of deities, arrived at what they presided as the perfect place for their creations. What would soon become, [[Tirathen]].

Thus the deities began their work. They formed the planes of existence, and created its denizens to preside over their domains.  Each plane acting as a layering against the fundamental forces of the universe. Creating the perfect place, right in the middle; The [[Material Plane]].  There, the deities began their work true, as they weaved their cosmic powers together, forming the stars, planets and moons. From the vast selection of planets, they chose one to call their first creation, [[Tirathen]].

At first, Tirathen was without true form, but deities struck at the planet, creating deep pits. They filled the pits with oceans, seas and lakes. They then rapidly heated and cooled the planet, until an atmosphere was formed. Then they created flora, filling the lands and seas with plants., that bore fruit and seed. The deities then created living things, creatures that fly, run, swim, and spring. Creatures that cry, call, bellow and howl. The creatures, were many and ate from each other, and the flora.

### The Eight Makers
Finally, the deities of Tirathen began to wonder about creating a sentient creature to fill the world. Many did not wish to create a sentient creature, believing they had already created a perfect world. While some few each had their own ideas about what to create, so these few decided that each would create one in secret, behind their siblings backs.

[[Asurion]], a deity who valued knowledge above all things, sought to create a creature that valued knowledge like himself, and were ever curious. Thus he ripped a page from his tome, and from the paper, he formed the first [[Gnome|Gnomes]], and he implanted sentience.

[[Barúm]], the deity that formed the lands of Tirathen, sought to create a creature that was of the earth. Thus he struck rock with his hammer, the stones and sparks that flew formed the first [[Dwarf|Dwarves]], and he implanted sentience.

[[Felurion]], the deity that created the stars and moon, sought to create a creature that captured the beauty and grace of a star. Thus she sang to the earth, and the trees danced and sung back, forming the first [[Elf|Elves]], and she implanted sentience.

[[Fenrith]], a deity who valued the creatures of the hunt and helped create the oceans, sought to create a creature that was a true ocean hunter. Thus he fired his arrow into the ocean, and from it was the first [[Athamaru]] born.

[[Orilyn]], a deity who valued the creatures of the land, and made the majority of them, believed that his creations were already perfect. Thus he implanted sentience in his favourite creatures, creating the first [[Beastfolk]].

[[Radia]], the deity who created the sun and the planes of existence, sought to create a creature who valued peace and love. Thus she wept, and from her tears she formed the first [[Halfling|Halflings]], and she implanted sentience.

[[Sarithar]], the deity who carried his siblings upon his back through the universe, sought to create a loyal creature in his image. Thus he hurled a scale down onto Tirathen, and from it were the first [[Kobold|Kobolds]] formed, and he implanted sentience.

[[Seralyn]], a deity who enjoyed tricking and toying with her siblings, sought to create a creature that embrace chaos. Thus she sliced her hand and bled into the earth, and from her blood were the first [[Goblin|Goblins]] formed, and she implanted sentience.

Thus were the primary Ancestries of the world formed by the Eight Makers.

### The Fracturing & The Founding of Religion
However, upon learning about some of their siblings creating sentient races, the rest of the deities were not pleased. A few saw their actions as unfortunate but remained friendly, while many viewed their actions as betrayal. They split themselves away from the Eight Makers and those that remained neutral. Forming their own pantheon of gods which would later become known as the [[Dark Pantheon]], while those that stuck by the Eight Makers formed what would be known as the [[Celestial Pantheon]].

While the two pantheons fought and argued amongst each other, life had begun to sprout on Tirathen, and the first religions were formed, but they did not worship the Celestial Gods, nor the Dark Gods. Instead, religion began by the worship of the elements, the great Titans of the Elemental Planes. This pantheon of Elemental Gods would later be known as the [[Old Pantheon]].

### The Dawn War
As mortal life began on Tirathen, something took notice. An ancient, unknowable creature from beyond [[The Great Beyond]], ripped and burrowed its way into the [[Material Plane]] made by the [[Gods|Deities]]. It came with only one goal in mind. To destroy and consume. [[Nhalgorath]], the Great Destroyer, consumed countless worlds within the [[Material Plane]] and relished in the taste, yet it only sought to consume more.

The [[Gods|Deities]], still bickering and fighting amongst each other were slow to notice [[Nhalgorath]]'s presence. That was until the Great Destroyer turned its gaze upon [[Tirathen]]. [[Radia]], the Great Mother was the first to notice the presence of [[Nhalgorath]] and rallied her siblings together. The Celestial & Dark Pantheon joining in unison once more to defend their all their creations from the Enemy of All. Their numerous clashes against [[Nhalgorath]] became known as [[The Dawn War]]

In the end, the [[Gods|Deities]] were victorious, yet [[Nhalgorath]] was unkillable. So they decided to seal the Destroyer within a specially constructed moon, [[Belegyr]]. [[Nhalgorath|Nhalgorath's]] defeat marked the end of the Dawn War, and the end of the Age of Creation.
