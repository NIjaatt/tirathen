The Marqir Union was a nation founded in the [[Shifting Sands]] region of [[Valloryn]] towards the end of [[the Age of Founding]]. It held a long and storied history, filled with unique culture and traditions unlike anywhere else in the lands. That was until [[the Age of Suffering]], and [[the Spellplague]]. 

Magic was a initially a boon to the people of Marqir who used it to benefit their people. They used magic to spring life from ground and create their paradise. In doing so, they infected not only themselves but the very sands of Marqir with the spellplague. As the people began to die, the sands came alive. Turning the desert into a sea of sand, sinking its cities, towns and villages and claiming the lives of many.

### Lands
In the present day, the lands of Marqir are unstable. The old capital Dahrim, lies buried beneath the shifting sands, lost forever. The only permanent settlement is [[Karreth]], the city of magic. Magically stabilised and floating above the sands that seek to swallow it.

### Culture
Little remains of Marqiri culture. What remains are nomadic wanderers that must constantly be on the move, lest they are swallowed up by the sands. Myths and oral history preserve fragments of the Marqir Union, but nothing whole.

For those that willingly remain in the Shifting Sands despite the danger, each wanderer holds their own beliefs about their stay.

Many believe that if the lost capital of Marqir is found, the Shifting Sands will settle, restoring stability and the glory of the old nation of Marqir. Others speak of a leader who will unite the wanderers, anchor the desert, and bring Marqir back to life.

While others are drawn to the myths and promises of old. Some believe abandoning the desert dishonours the memory of their Marqiri ancestors, for the desert itself is sacred.

And some wanderers are simply stubborn and refuse to leave. These typically believe the act of surviving the sands is a rite both physical, and spiritual in nature. Life elsewhere would be easy, but meaningless without their connection to the sands.

Funnily enough, this last type of wanderers make for the best Sandspeakers. Guides who lead people through the Shifting Sands for weeks or months at a time. They claim to be able to commune with the sand.
