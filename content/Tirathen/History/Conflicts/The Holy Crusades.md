The Holy Crusades refer to three Crusades undertaken by the [[Halmyr|Holy Kingdom of Halmyr]], the seat of Divine worship on Tirathen. The First Crusade refers to Halmyr's campaign against the Demonic invasion through the Darkrift. The Second Crusade refers to Halmyr's campaign against the Human invaders and their poisonous magic. The Third Crusade refers to Halmyr's campaign against the Lich King, Tyr Al'Bahir.

### The First Crusade 961 ~ 968 P.D
In 937 P.D, The Holy Kingdom of Halmyr launched a campaign to purge a cult dedicated to a demon lord. By 940 P.D, they had rooted the heretics out, and successfully defending the Holy Kingdom from demonic influence.

However, the cult was not finished as they reorganized themselves in the [[Taldar|Kingdom of Taldar]], just to the north of Halmyr. Some few years later, a witch, working in conjunction with her demonic patron, opened [[The Darkrift]]. Demons poured out, and overran the nation of Taldar. As the rift grew everlarger, it came to be seen as a threat to the entire world. Thus began the First Crusade.

Even though warriors had been traveling to Taldar to battle the demonic hordes ever since the Darkrift opened in 945 P.D, the First Crusade was not named so by the Holy Kingdom until 961 P.D. Its "late" start can be attributed to the general environmental and social upheaval caused by the mass appearance of Demons worldwide.

The crusaders discovered upon their arrival, that the Kingdom of Taldar had fallen, and parts of western [[Myrathin]] had already been overrun. The nations of [[Dunvaar]], [[Valdyr]], and Myrathin, each immediately threatened by the demonic invasion, joined the Holy Kingdom in their efforts.

Their numbers and organizational strengths added greatly to the defending armies, and soon the demonic forces began to be pushed back into Taldar. Having thus liberated all of the surrounding regions outside of Taldar, they attempted to push further into Taldar only to be met with fierce resistance.

After five years of attempting to push into Taldar, Pope Ikanos decreed that Taldar and the demons would be sealed. Great wardstones were forged by hand of the Dwarves of Dunvaar, carved by the Elves of Valdyr, and imbued with Halmyr's divine powers. These great wardstones were placed all along the borders of Taldar, each one connected to it's neighbouring stones. Creating a barrier that kept the demonic forces from passing. These wardstones would come to be known as the [[Gates of Ikanos]].

Thus did the First Crusade end in 968 P.D, though some do not believe the First Crusade was a victory, having conceded Taldar to demon kind.

### The Second Crusade 1211 P.D ~ 1224 P.D
In 1202 P.D, Humans arrived on Tirathen from another world. Bringing with them magic, which infected the land and the native denizens of Tirathen like a plague.

The Holy Kingdom of Halmyr saw Humans as dark, unholy invaders, and began their campaign in 1211 P.D. They marched through the [[Shifting Sands]] and invaded [[Korvia]] where Humans had taken root.

The crusaders clashed with the Humans at the Spine of Drougatha, and eventually managed to push their way into Korvia. However, [[The Spellplague]] proved to be much more deadly than the world first anticipated as Halmyr's armies were falling rapidly to the poisonous magic. Despite being a mighty ground force, there was naught Halmyr could do in the face of the magic wielding humans. The crusaders were pushed out of Korvia, and forced to return the Halmyr. Thus ending the Second Crusade in 1224 P.D.

### The Third Crusade  618 ~ 691 A.C
In the 8th century P.D, a powerful mercenary terrorised the lands of [[Valdyr]]. Dubbed, the Mercenary King, Tyr al'Bahir. An immensely powerful warrior, and expert general who commanded a relatively small army of elite soldiers. No one knows how the Mercenary King died, but historians believe it was somewhere between 850 ~ 870 P.D. 

Through the power of magic, he was reborn in 618 A.C, now calling himself the Lich King. Gathering both alive and undead followers, he travelled from [[Ardia]] to the [[Frozen North]]. There he unified the quarrelling tribes by promising them the riches of the southern lands. By the end of that year, they had conquered almost all of [[Korvia]] and brought its people under Tyr al'Bahir's control.

Warriors from all over the empire rallied, converging on [[Korvia]] in defence of mortal life, led by the Holy Kingdom of Halmyr. Over the following decades, they fought valiantly against the Lich King, winning a number of conflicts. Yet the Crusade was not that simple, as the forces of the Lich King seemed endless, as any living soldier who fell in battle could simply be raised as an undead thrall and rejoin the fray. Every battle that Tyr al'Bahir joined ended in a humiliating defeat for Halmyr.

In the final battle of the war in 691 A.C, the crusaders faced an overwhelming undead army. Tyr al'Bahir cast terrible magics upon his enemies, singling out the great General Ozani. The General was protected by a mighty artifact known as the Shield of Aeyora, which absorbed the magic and then broke into a dozen pieces. One of these pieces embedded itself into the lich's hand and the Lich King burned with a holy fire. Incapacitated, the lich's soul returned to the phylactery beneath his fortress-tower of [[Darkspire]], within the lands of Ardia. The final battle continued in Ardia outside Darkspire as the Lich King's allies tried their best to buy time until he awakened once more. However, without their great commander, they were unable to hold back the united forces of Tirathen. Unfortunately, it was quickly discovered that no mortal could destroy the lich, so the crusaders placed a great seal upon Darkspire, beneath which Tyr al'Bahir would be eternally entombed, unable to rise again as long as the seal remained in tact.

Any living tribe folk who survived the lich's defeat fled back to the Frozen North, where they fell back into tribal infighting. The former living slaves of the immortal tyrant were returned to their homelands, freeing the peoples of Korvia after nearly a century of servitude. Many of the lich-king's top lieutenants, Vampires, Demons, Devils, Werecreatures and other dark creatures fled rather than face the final death on the battlefield.

With the Lich King's defeat, and the scattering of his armies, thus did the Third Crusade come to an end in 691 A.C.

Yet the effects of this crusade still affect the present day. As all the peoples who aided or joined the forces of Tyr Al'Bahir while still alive had their last names forgotten, and were branded with the last name Al'Bahir. Their families were also branded with the Al'Bahir name, and their descendants. There aren't many Al'Bahir traitors in the current age, most of which turn to lives of crime as they are turned away from opportunities due to their name.
