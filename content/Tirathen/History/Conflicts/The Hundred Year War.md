The Hundred Year War, also known as the Wars of Expansion was a century-long war of conquest launched by the recently arrived Humans to increase their holdings. It begun in 1298 P.D in [[Korvia]] and eventually expanded control into territories now known as the collectively as the Westlands and Heartlands. The Hundred Year War lasted through the reigns of six Human monarchs and finally ended in 1391 P.D.

### The War
The Humans first set their sights to the north, claiming land in what is now [[Orahen]], [[Kalaen]], and [[Thars]]. The nation of Thars resisted the longest against the Human invaders for decades, but finally surrendered to them in 1331 P.D.

With Thars out of the way, the conquest moved further north yet. With a second Human nation forming within the lands of [[Valdyr]] and declaring independence from Korvia. Which the Korvian Emperor at the time did not like. The Emperor of the time, Athelmyr I, overran a large section of Valdyr, cutting off their trade routes with the North. Much to the surprise Athelmyr I, Valdyr proved much more of a challenge than originally thought. After their lands were initially captured, Valdyr retreated back into the [[Heartlands]] of their country to the east. Securing themselves within Aeyora's Pass in 1351 P.D with a Fortress named [[Fort Gerson]]. A position Valdyr has successfully held until the end of the Hundred Year War, and to the present day.

The Korvian Empire also attempted to set a foothold into the [[Frozen North]] in 1380 P.D. After spending a decade there, they ultimately found the lands there too unruly and wild to colonize.

### End of the Hundred Year War
The Hundred Year War finally came to a close in 1391 P.D when the Korvian crown ceded its territorial ambitions in [[Valdyr]] and the [[Frozen North]].
