The War of the Lions was a conflict between the Korvian Empire and the Kingdom of Valdyr that lasted 20 years, starting with the Valdyrian Offensive in 1320 A.C, and ending with the settlement of an uneasy peace in 1341 A.C.

### History
In the early 1300s A.C, the Korvian Empire suffered from internal rebellion and upheaval. The threat was fierce enough that many of the armies of Korvia were called south to pacify the rebellious regions and nobles. Leaving the Northern regions of Korvia unprotected from serious threat. The Korvian Empire at the time was lead by Emperor Cassius Baragav IV, who was known as the Lion of the West. Emperor Baragav IV had a number of sons, but only one Crown Heir who was [[Aedric Baragav II|Aedric II]], also known as the Young Lion for his similarities to his father.

In the Kingdom of Valdyr, the throne had been seized by the younger of two brothers, [[Thalagar Rathven|Thalagar]], after a bloody battle of succession. Thalagar was also known as the Lion of the East. Eager to leave his mark on the world, King Thalagar was quick to exploit the chance to reclaim the lost lands of [[Valdyr]], and strike at the weakened Korvian Empire.

In 1320 A.C, King Thalagar marched his armies of 10,000 across Aeyora's Pass and into Korvian lands. Capturing the city of Derrovan. They burned towns and enslaved the people virtually unopposed. Within a very short period of time, King Thalagar had reclaimed most of Northern Korvia's lands and were only stopped by the natural barrier of the River Afonir in 1322 A.C. This rapid seizing of land is recorded in Valdyrian history as the Thalven Victories, and in Korvia as the Thalven Massacres.

The rapid progress of Thalagar's armies terrified the nobles and peasants of Korvia, who withdrew south towards the capital, Ashkareth. Cassius IV was politically pressured to ride north to confront the King of Valdyr, and so he did. Their forces met in 1323 A.C, and were swiftly defeated by King Thalagar who captured the Emperor and held him ransom, asking for the rightful return of the Northern Lands to Valdyr.

The Korvian Royal Family and the Four Ducal Houses quickly voted to strip Cassius IV of his monarchy, and instated his firstborn son, Aedric II as the new Emperor of Korvia who also inherited his father's title as Lion of the West. Under Aedric II's rule, Korvia did not cede their Northern Lands to Valdyr despite King Thalagar's demands.

A year after the capture of Cassius IV, King Thalagar and his forces set fire to the city of [[Derrovan]] in 1324 A.C in an event known as the Red Night, and sent the mutilated carcass of Cassius IV to Ashkareth. This massacre, and the mistreatment of their former Emperor is still used by Korvians in the present to justify their hatred of their north-eastern neighbour. Meanwhile, King Thalagar returned home the [[Valdyr]].

### The Counterattack
The Young Lion, Aedric II proved his worth as emperor, as he defended attacks and provocations from the northern forces of King Thalagar, while simultaneously ending the rebellion against his rule. Three years after the Red Night, Aedric II was ready to take the offensive. He personally led his armies north in a campaign known as the Heaven's March in 1327 A.C.

After defeating six Valdyrian generals on the Plains of Thars, Aedric II regained control of the northern lands, reinstating the old borders in 1330 A.C.

King Thalagar returned to the war after a year in 1331 A.C and took command of the [[Heirs Keep]], a fortress within Aeyora's Pass. While Aedric II occupied the [[Dragon's Wall]], a fortress also located within Aeyora's Pass.

The armies of Korvia and Valdyr clashed many times within the Pass over the following decades. Slowly the name of Aeyora's Pass faded, instead becoming known as the Bloody Pass.

### Uneasy Peace
In 1340 A.C, both monarchs were away from the Bloody Pass. With Aedric II in [[Ashkareth]], and Thalagar in [[Eryndor]].

Emperor Aedric II had been dealing with numerous rebellions over the years during the War, which he had always dealt with easily. However, during the summer of 1340 one of his own Ducal houses attempted a coup, forcing Aedric II to withdraw troops from the north. That, paired with [[The Great Northern Exodus]] ongoing, Aedric II had his hands full.

At the same time, King Thalagar, had secured a political marriage between one of the high ranking families of the [[Halmyr|Holy Kingdom of Halmyr]] and his only son and heir, Lewys. Finally, his years of strengthening his ties of Halmyr's political and military might was close. Yet in one drunken night, he assaulted his future daughter-in-law and was caught in the act by his son. Lewys, outraged split from his father and defected further east, taking his fiancé with him along with many nobles, merchants and knights. Lewys split the Kingdom of Valdyr in two, and formed the [[Elovan|Elovantite Principality]].

With both Lions facing intense political stress and turmoil back home, they simultaneously came to an agreement to cease the war in 1341 A.C.
